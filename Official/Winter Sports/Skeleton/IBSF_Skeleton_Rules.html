Section 1: Introduction

1.1 Overview and Governing Body

Skeleton returned to the Olympic program at the 2002 Salt Lake City Games after a 54-year absence. Governed by the IBSF under the IBSF International Rules, athletes race head-first in a prone position (face down) on a small sled down the same tracks used for bobsled and luge. Olympic events include Men's and Women's individual competitions. The sport is distinguished from luge by the head-first orientation and running start, with athletes reaching speeds exceeding 140 km/h (87 mph).

Section 2: Equipment

2.1 Required Equipment

  • Sled: Maximum weight: 43 kg (94.8 lb) men, 35 kg (77.2 lb) women. Maximum combined weight (sled + athlete): 120 kg (264.6 lb) men, 92 kg (202.8 lb) women. Maximum length 120 cm (47.2 in). Maximum width 38 cm (15 in). Steel runners.
  • Helmet: Chin guard helmet mandatory, meeting IBSF specifications. Must provide chin and face protection due to head-first orientation.
  • Suit: Skin-tight suit. Maximum thickness regulated. No aerodynamic aids.
  • Shoes: Spike shoes for running start. Maximum spike length 7 mm (0.28 in).

Section 3: Playing Area

3.1 Venue Specifications

Races held on artificially refrigerated tracks (shared with bobsled and luge).

  • Track length: 1200–1650 m (3937–5413 ft)
  • Vertical drop: 100–150 m (328–492 ft)
  • Athletes use the same track as bobsled (full-length start)
  • Running start zone: approximately 30–40 m (98–131 ft)

Section 4: Players & Officials

4.1 Competitors and Officials

Individual competition. Jury: Technical Delegate, Race Director. Electronic timing to 1/100th second. Start time, split times, and finish time recorded.

Section 5: Rules of Play

5.1 Core Rules

Competition format: 4 runs over 2 days (2 runs per day). Combined time determines final ranking.

Start: athlete sprints while pushing the sled for approximately 30–40 m, then dives head-first onto the sled.

Athlete steers by shifting body weight and applying pressure with shoulders, knees, and toes to the sled.

Athlete must cross the finish line on the sled. Separating from the sled before the finish results in a DNF.

Sled inspection including runner temperature and weight conducted before and after each run.

Section 6: Scoring

6.1 Scoring System

Combined time of all 4 runs determines final ranking. Times recorded to 1/100th second.

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

7.1 Infractions and Consequences

  • Runner temperature exceeds limit: DSQ for that run
  • Sled or combined weight exceeds maximum: DSQ
  • Equipment modification after inspection: DSQ
  • Athlete not on sled at finish: DNF

Section 8: Safety Considerations

8.1 Safety Protocols

Chin guard helmet mandatory due to head-first position. Track padding at high-impact zones. Medical team at finish area. Speed monitoring. Track inspection before each session.
