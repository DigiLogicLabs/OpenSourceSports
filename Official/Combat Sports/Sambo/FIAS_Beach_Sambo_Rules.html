Section 1: Introduction

Beach Sambo is the outdoor sand-court variant of Sport Sambo developed and governed by FIAS. Designed to extend Sambo's competitive footprint into beach-sport and multi-sport festival programming (e.g., ANOC World Beach Games), Beach Sambo adapts the Sport Sambo ruleset for sand-surface competition with shortened match length and modified throw-scoring to account for the softer landing surface. See sibling Sport Sambo and Combat Sambo entries for the indoor mat-based variants.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Sambo jacket + shorts (red/blue per competitor) — same as Sport Sambo
  • Barefoot (no Sambovki required on sand)
  • Mouthguard recommended; protective cup permitted (men)
  • No jewelry; nails trimmed; hair tied back
  • Sun-protection (visor/headband) permitted; subject to referee approval

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Sand court: 10m × 10m FIAS-spec compacted-but-soft sand area
  • Minimum sand depth: ~30 cm
  • Marked boundary tape or cord
  • Shaded competitor and official rest areas adjacent to court

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Two competitors per match
  • Beach Sambo weight classes typically consolidated to 3-4 categories per gender (vs. 7 indoor)
  • Officials: 1 mat referee + 1 corner judge + scoring official + timekeeper

Section 5: Rules of Play

  • Match length: 3 minutes (single period; vs 4-min indoor Sport Sambo)
  • Throws scored as in Sport Sambo (1, 2, or 4 points based on landing position)
  • Groundwork: pin-time reduced to account for sand difficulty (3-second pin = 1 pt; 6-second = 2 pts)
  • Joint locks on arms legal; knees + ankles legal at Senior level
  • No chokes
  • Submission = total victory
  • 10-point differential = technical superiority + total victory

Section 6: Scoring

  • Decision priority: total victory > technical superiority > points > last-scoring competitor
  • Tournament: single-elimination + repechage
  • Gold/Silver/two Bronze per weight + gender

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Sand surface reduces blunt-impact injury risk vs indoor mat (softer landing), but increases ankle/knee instability injuries from uneven footing during throws. Mandatory pre-event medical screening. Heat-stress protocols for outdoor competition (mandatory water breaks, heat-stop authority, shaded rest areas). Sand-temperature monitoring (max-temperature thresholds for outdoor events). On-site athletic trainer + EMT + ambulance at FIAS Beach Sambo events. AED on-site. Sun exposure protocols: shaded venues preferred; sunscreen permitted between matches. Concussion protocol per FIAS medical guidelines.
