Section 1: Introduction

The Ju-Jitsu International Federation (JJIF) is the IOC-recognized international governing body for traditional Japanese Ju-Jitsu (distinct from Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu). JJIF administers three competitive disciplines: Fighting, Ne-Waza (groundwork), and Duo (paired forms). The Fighting discipline is JJIF's signature mixed-rules combat format, which divides each match into three phases: striking (Phase 1), throws + transitions (Phase 2), and groundwork (Phase 3) — a structure that showcases the complete Ju-Jitsu skill set. See sibling JJIF Ne-Waza Rules entry for the groundwork-only ruleset.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Gi (Kimono): JJIF-approved white gi (Phase 2 + 3); red/blue belt for distinguishing competitors
  • Hand pads / open-fingered gloves: for Phase 1 striking — lightly padded, similar to amateur boxing
  • Headgear: mandatory in Junior categories; optional in Senior
  • Mouthguard: mandatory
  • Shin guards + instep pads: mandatory
  • Groin protector: mandatory (men); chest protector for women optional
  • Barefoot competition

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Two competitors per match
  • Weight classes follow JJIF schedule (Men: -56, -62, -69, -77, -85, -94, +94 kg; Women similar consolidation)
  • Age categories: Cadet, Junior, Senior, Veteran
  • Officials: 1 mat referee + 2 corner judges + scoring official + timekeeper
  • JJIF-certified referee panel with continental rotation

Section 5: Rules of Play

Match Length

  • Single 3-minute period (4 minutes for Senior major championship)
  • Match is continuous; the three "phases" describe the techniques permitted at any given moment, not separate timed sub-rounds

Phase 1: Striking (Atemi-Waza)

  • Light-contact punches + kicks to permitted target areas
  • 1 point per clean strike
  • No knockouts permitted — heavy contact is penalized

Phase 2: Throws + Transitions

  • Throws scored 2-7 points depending on amplitude + landing
  • Ippon (full throw to back): 7 points
  • Wazari (partial back-landing): 5 points
  • Yuko: 3 points

Phase 3: Groundwork (Newaza)

  • Pin (15 seconds): 5 points
  • Submission via tap = total victory (ippon)
  • Joint locks on arms + chokes legal at Senior level; restricted in Junior
  • No leg locks (heel hooks, kneebars prohibited)

Phase Transitions

  • Continuous flow: referee directs transitions based on competitor actions
  • Stalemate in any phase → referee restart in standing

Section 6: Scoring

  • Decision priority: ippon/submission > points > referee decision
  • Tournament: single-elimination + repechage
  • Gold/Silver/two Bronze per weight + gender + age

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Heavy contact in Phase 1 (striking too hard): warning → point deduction → DQ
  • Illegal techniques (eye-poke, throat, spine, head-butts, leg locks, etc.): caution → DQ
  • Stalling / passivity: warning → caution → opponent advantage
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: DQ
  • Anti-doping: WADA Code (JJIF signatory); in-competition + out-of-competition testing

Section 8: Safety Considerations

JJIF Fighting's light-contact striking rule (vs full-contact alternatives) substantially reduces concussion risk. Mandatory headgear for Junior categories adds protection. Phase 2 throws + Phase 3 groundwork carry standard combat-grappling injury risk (shoulder, knee, neck strain). JJIF requires on-site team physician + EMT + ambulance at international championship level. AED on-site. Concussion protocol: SCAT-5 assessment + multi-stage return-to-competition clearance. Skin-infection screening before mat use. Equipment inspection pre-match.
