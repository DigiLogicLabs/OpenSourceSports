Section 1: Introduction

Abu Dhabi Jiu-Jitsu Pro (AJP) is the professional jiu-jitsu circuit operated by the UAE Jiu-Jitsu Federation (UAEJJF). AJP rose to prominence by operating the Abu Dhabi World Professional Championship (the highest-prize-purse BJJ tournament in the world) and an extensive Grand Slam circuit with stops on every inhabited continent. AJP rules are similar to but distinct from IBJJF — AJP uses its own scoring schedule, weight-class distribution, and submission legality rules. AJP and IBJJF are the two dominant rule-sets in competitive sport BJJ. See the sibling IBJJF Rules entry for the alternative international ruleset.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Gi (Kimono): AJP-approved gi; colored belt indicating rank
  • No-Gi: rashguard + shorts/grappling pants
  • Gi colors: white, blue, or black
  • Mouthguard recommended; protective cup permitted
  • No shoes, no jewelry, nails trimmed
  • Female competitors: rashguard required under gi top in some events

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Standard 8m × 8m AJP-approved tatami; multiple mats at Grand Slam events

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Two competitors per match
  • Weight classes (Adult Male, kg): -56, -62, -69, -77, -85, -94, -120
  • Belt classification: White, Blue, Purple, Brown, Black (recognized via UAEJJF + IBJJF cross-recognition)
  • Age categories: Juvenile, Adult, Masters
  • Officials: 1 mat referee + 1 scoring official + 1 timekeeper; AJP-certified referee panel

Section 5: Rules of Play

Match Length

  • White Belt: 4 min; Blue: 5 min; Purple: 6 min; Brown: 7 min; Black: 8 min (10 min for major tournaments)
  • If tied: referee decision based on points, advantages, penalties

AJP Scoring

  • 2 points: Takedown, Sweep, Knee-on-Belly
  • 3 points: Guard Pass
  • 4 points: Mount, Back Mount
  • Scoring schedule differs subtly from IBJJF (e.g., AJP counts takedown as 2 like sweep; IBJJF does similarly)

Submissions

  • Joint locks + chokes legal subject to belt + age restrictions
  • Heel hooks legal in no-gi at adult Brown/Black at some AJP events (more permissive than IBJJF)
  • Knee reaps legal in some no-gi categories
  • Submission via tap, verbal submission, or referee stoppage = instant win

Penalties + Advantages

  • Stalling, fleeing match, illegal grip → penalties
  • 4 penalties = DQ
  • Advantage awarded for near-completion of scoring moves; tiebreaker only

Section 6: Scoring

  • Decision priority: submission > points > advantages > penalties > referee decision
  • AJP Tour points: each Grand Slam round contributes ranking points; year-end championship tour standings
  • World Pro Championship: highest annual purse in BJJ (~$50K+ for gold)
  • Gold/Silver/Bronze per weight + belt + age

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Stalling: verbal warning → penalty; repeated = DQ
  • Fleeing: exiting match area without tactical reason = penalty
  • Illegal submissions for belt/age: immediate DQ if applied
  • Slamming: DQ
  • Gi-grip violations: grabbing inside cuffs/pants → penalty
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: DQ
  • Anti-doping: AJP anti-doping program; in-event + out-of-competition testing

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Same injury profile as BJJ generally: joint injuries (shoulder, elbow, knee), occasional concussion. AJP belt-graded submission restrictions enforced; high-risk submissions (heel hooks, knee reaps, twisters) only legal at higher belt levels in specific no-gi categories. Slamming prohibited. Referee stops for safety. On-site EMT + medical doctor at Grand Slam and World Pro level; AED on-site. Skin-infection screening before mat use. Concussion protocol per AJP medical guidelines. Pre-match medical check.
