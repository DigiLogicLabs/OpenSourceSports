Section 1: Introduction

Ne-Waza ("ground-technique") is JJIF's groundwork-only competition discipline, structurally similar to Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu but governed under the IOC-recognized Ju-Jitsu International Federation. JJIF Ne-Waza covers both gi and no-gi categories with a points-based scoring system and submissions as instant wins. The discipline provides a competition pathway for traditional Japanese Ju-Jitsu practitioners alongside the more striking-oriented JJIF Fighting format. See sibling IBJJF Rules, AJP Jiu-Jitsu Rules, and JJIF Fighting Rules entries for related rule-sets in the broader jiu-jitsu ecosystem.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Gi (Kimono): JJIF-approved white gi for Gi category; colored belt + red/blue sash for distinguishing competitors
  • No-Gi: rashguard + shorts; rashguard color (red/blue) for competitor distinction
  • Mouthguard recommended; protective cup permitted (men)
  • No shoes, no jewelry, nails trimmed

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Weight classes follow JJIF schedule
  • Age categories: Cadet, Junior, Senior, Veteran
  • Officials: 1 mat referee + 2 corner judges + scoring official + timekeeper

Section 5: Rules of Play

Match Length

  • 5 minutes (single period)
  • If tied: 2-minute golden-score overtime, first scoring action wins

Starting Position

  • Match begins standing; both competitors approach + engage
  • Pulling guard permitted but referee may intervene if continuous pulling without action

Scoring

  • 2 points: Takedown, Sweep, Knee-on-Belly
  • 3 points: Guard Pass
  • 4 points: Mount, Back Mount
  • Submission: instant win

Submissions

  • Joint locks legal on arms; some on legs (varies by belt + age)
  • Chokes legal at Senior level
  • No heel hooks, no twisters, no neck cranks (more restrictive than ADCC or modern no-gi grappling)

Section 6: Scoring

  • Decision priority: submission > points > golden score > referee decision
  • Tournament: single-elimination + repechage
  • Gold/Silver/two Bronze per weight + gender + age + format (gi vs no-gi)

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Stalling: verbal warning → caution → opponent advantage
  • Illegal submissions: caution → DQ
  • Slamming: DQ
  • Fleeing match: caution
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: DQ
  • Anti-doping: WADA Code (JJIF signatory)

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Ne-Waza injury profile: joint injuries (shoulder, elbow, knee), neck strain, occasional concussion. JJIF restricts high-risk submissions (heel hooks, twisters) more strictly than ADCC or even IBJJF. Slamming prohibited. JJIF requires on-site team physician + EMT + ambulance at international championship level. AED on-site. Concussion protocol: SCAT-5 assessment + multi-stage return-to-competition clearance. Skin-infection screening before mat use. Equipment inspection pre-match.
