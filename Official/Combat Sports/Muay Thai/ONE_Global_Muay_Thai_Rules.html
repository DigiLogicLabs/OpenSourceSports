Section 1: Introduction

Muay Thai under ONE Championship is the modernized professional ruleset operated by the Singapore-based ONE FC promotion (founded by Chatri Sityodtong in 2011). ONE's Muay Thai division uses 4-ounce MMA-style open-fingered gloves (lighter than traditional 8-ounce boxing gloves used in Thailand), 3 rounds × 3 minutes (vs Thai-traditional 5 rounds × 3 minutes), and Western-influenced 10-point-must scoring with emphasis on damage + ring generalship. The result is a more knockout-friendly format that has helped grow Muay Thai's global pro fight scene. ONE Championship sits alongside Thai-traditional venues like Lumpinee, Rajadamnern, and World Muaythai Council (WMC) events in the global pro Muay Thai ecosystem.

Section 2: Equipment

  • 4-oz MMA-style fingerless gloves — open palm (distinct from traditional 8-10oz Thai boxing gloves)
  • Mouthguard: mandatory
  • Groin protector: mandatory (men); chest protector for women
  • No shin guards in professional matches
  • Mongkol (sacred headband, removed before fighting): traditional Thai pre-fight blessing element
  • Prajied (arm bands): traditional, kept on during fight
  • Short trunks; barefoot competition
  • Wai Khru (pre-fight dance) is honored at ONE Championship's Thai-spirit-event format

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Ring: square boxing-style ring (typically 6m × 6m to 7m × 7m), 4 roped sides, padded canvas floor
  • Distinct from MMA cage — fighters bounce off ropes
  • Mandatory padded turnbuckle corners
  • Ring elevation and lighting per arena standard

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Two competitors per match
  • Weight classes (Men, kg): Strawweight, Flyweight, Bantamweight, Featherweight, Lightweight, Welterweight, Middleweight, Light Heavyweight, Heavyweight (ONE-specific scaling)
  • Women's divisions: Atomweight, Strawweight, Flyweight, Bantamweight, Featherweight
  • Officials: 1 in-ring referee + 3 judges + ringside physician + ONE Championship commission inspector

Section 5: Rules of Play

Match Length

  • 3 rounds × 3 minutes (60-second rest between rounds)
  • Championship fights: 5 rounds × 3 minutes

Eight Limbs of Muay Thai

  • Punches (closed fist)
  • Kicks (legs, body, head)
  • Knee strikes (clinch + flying)
  • Elbow strikes (clinch range)

Clinch

  • Clinch fighting permitted — fundamental to Muay Thai
  • Knees + elbows in clinch are legal and heavily scored
  • Sweeps from clinch (without slamming) are legal

Permitted Sweeps

  • Foot sweeps + reaps to bring opponent down (with feet only, no judo throws)
  • Holding+slamming explicitly prohibited

Knockdowns + Knockouts

  • Standing 10-count for knockdowns
  • 3-knockdown rule per round: 3 knockdowns = TKO
  • KO + TKO via stoppage = total victory

Section 6: Scoring

  • 10-Point Must scoring (boxing-style)
  • Judges weight: damage dealt, ring generalship, clean strikes, aggression, defense
  • Distinct from Thai-traditional scoring (which weights ring-walking, technique purity, dominance late in the fight); ONE's Westernized scoring favors knockout-attempting aggression
  • Championship titles awarded per weight class; ONE Muay Thai World Champion belt

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Illegal strikes: head-butts, back-of-head, throat, spine, eye-poke, groin → warning → point deduction → DQ
  • Slamming opponent from clinch: DQ
  • Hitting on the break: warning → point deduction
  • Holding-and-hitting (excessive): warning → break
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct / corner-man interference: point deduction → DQ
  • Anti-doping: ONE Championship anti-doping policy; WADA-aligned testing

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Muay Thai is full-contact 8-limb striking with elevated injury risk (KO, lacerations, fractures, joint trauma from kicks/elbows). ONE Championship mandates: comprehensive pre-fight medical (neurological, cardiac, ophthalmological, MRI for major bouts), ringside physician with stoppage authority, on-site EMT + ambulance + medical team, AED in arena. Post-fight medical assessment. KO triggers automatic medical suspension (minimum 30-60 days, neurological clearance required). Concussion protocol per ONE medical guidelines. Mandatory weigh-in + IV-fluid prohibition (ONE was first major promotion to ban dangerous weight-cutting practices). Hydration testing + day-of-fight weight checks.
