Section 1: Introduction

The Canadian Armwrestling Federation (CAWF) is the national governing body for amateur armwrestling in Canada and the recognized WAF national affiliate. CAWF organizes the annual Canadian National Armwrestling Championships and a network of provincial qualifiers (Ontario Armwrestling Association, Alberta Armwrestling Association, etc.). CAWF rules are WAF-aligned with Canadian-specific event structure, weight-class age-grade divisions, and a developmental pathway for under-18 athletes. See the sibling WAF Armwrestling Rules entry for the international ruleset.

Section 2: Equipment

  • CAWF endorses WAF-spec table (104 cm tall × 91-93 cm long; padded elbow pads + pegs + touch pads)
  • Mandated short-sleeve shirt (mid-bicep maximum); no jewelry; trimmed nails
  • Indoor sport shoes; chalk permitted at championship level
  • Provincial-event tables must meet WAF specifications + CAWF table-inspection certification

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Two competitors per match (1-on-1)
  • Weight classes inherit from WAF (with optional CAWF-specific adjustments for junior development categories)
  • Age categories: Sub-Junior, Junior, Senior, Masters, Grand Masters; Para and Open divisions
  • Officials: 1 main referee + 1 side referee (2-up system); CAWF-certified referees only at sanctioned events
  • Provincial referee certification with national renewal cycle

Section 5: Rules of Play

  • Starting position: elbows on pads, non-pulling hand grips peg, foot on floor; referee centers grip
  • Referee starts match with "Ready... Go!"
  • Win condition: pin opponent's hand to touch pad
  • Match = best of 3 rounds at championship; single round at preliminary
  • 90-second per-round time limit; judges score if no pin
  • Foul system: 1st = warning, 2nd = round loss
  • Common technique categories: top-roll, hook, press — all permitted within positional rules

Section 6: Scoring

  • 1 point per round won; best of 3 wins match
  • Single-elimination bracket with double-loss elimination (repechage)
  • Gold/Silver/Bronze per weight class per arm per age category
  • Provincial points feed into national qualification

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Standard WAF foul taxonomy: elbow off pad, shoulder off-line, foot off floor, false start, grip break
  • Anti-doping: Canadian Centre for Ethics in Sport (CCES) testing program; WADA Code aligned
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: disqualification
  • Equipment violation (illegal sleeve, jewelry): warning + correction; refusal = DQ

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Same primary injury risk as WAF: spiral humerus fractures, shoulder strain, elbow ligament damage. CAWF mandates competitor education on safe arm posture; referees authorized to halt for breaks in arm position. On-site athletic trainer required at provincial qualifiers; full medical team + ambulance at Canadian Nationals. Concussion protocol unnecessary in this sport (no head contact). Pre-competition medical clearance for Masters+ and Para divisions. AED on-site at championship-level events. Mandatory junior-development warm-up protocol.
