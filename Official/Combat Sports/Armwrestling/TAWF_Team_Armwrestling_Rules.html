Section 1: Introduction

The Team Armwrestling World Federation (TAWF) governs the team format of international armwrestling, in which entire national teams compete head-to-head across multiple weight classes for aggregated team scoring rather than individual medals. TAWF builds on WAF technical rules but applies a relay-style format: each nation fields one competitor per weight class, the cumulative team score across all weight-class matchups determines the team winner. The format is designed to grow armwrestling's spectator appeal through nation-vs-nation competition. See the sibling WAF Armwrestling Rules and CAWF Armwrestling Rules entries for individual-format rules.

Section 2: Equipment

  • WAF-spec armwrestling table (104 cm tall, 91-93 cm long, padded elbow pads + pegs + touch pads)
  • Team kit: each nation wears a coordinated uniform with national crest
  • Standard apparel: short-sleeve shirt (mid-bicep), no jewelry, trimmed nails
  • Indoor sport shoes

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Central championship table with elevated platform for spectator/broadcast visibility
  • Team benches flanking the table for each competing nation
  • Warm-up area with practice tables; team medical staff station

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Each team fields one competitor per weight class (typically 5-on-5 or 7-on-7 depending on event format)
  • Standard WAF weight classes (Men: 70/80/90/100/+100 kg; Women: 60/70/+70 kg in 5v5 simplified format)
  • Team captain + non-competing coach allowed in coach's box
  • Officials: 1 main referee + 1 side referee at each match; chief referee per session; head referee per championship

Section 5: Rules of Play

  • Each weight-class matchup follows WAF individual rules (best-of-3 rounds, pin to touch pad)
  • Matches contested sequentially: lowest weight class first, then ascending
  • Score updates posted live for team standings
  • Each weight-class winner awards 1 point to their team; tied weight-class = 0.5 each
  • Team with most aggregated points wins the dual
  • Tournament format: round-robin pool play + knockout semifinals + final

Section 6: Scoring

  • 1 point per weight-class win (within team dual)
  • 0.5 points per tied weight class
  • Team with majority of weight-class points wins the dual
  • Tiebreaker (if duals tied): aggregate match-time, then aggregate cumulative round wins
  • Tournament: gold/silver/bronze for teams + MVP individual award per weight class

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • WAF individual foul taxonomy applies (elbow off pad, shoulder off-line, foot off floor)
  • Team-level penalties: coach interference outside coach's box → team warning, then point deduction
  • Equipment violation: warning + correction; refusal = individual DQ + 0 to team for that weight class
  • Anti-doping: WADA Code aligned; WAF-administered testing program

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Same individual injury risk as WAF: spiral humerus fractures, shoulder strain, elbow ligament damage. TAWF team format adds team-medical-staff requirement on each bench. Referees authorized to halt for breaks in arm position. On-site full medical team + ambulance at TAWF World Cup. AED at championship table. Pre-competition cardiac screening for Masters team members. Junior development teams subject to additional protective protocols (warm-up minimums, age-cap on match count per session).
