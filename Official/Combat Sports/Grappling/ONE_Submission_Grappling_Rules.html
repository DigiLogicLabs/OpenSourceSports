Section 1: Introduction

ONE Championship's Submission Grappling division is a professional grappling super-fight format launched in 2021 to provide elite BJJ + grappling competitors with sustained pay scale, regular event coverage, and a globally televised platform alongside ONE's MMA + Muay Thai + Kickboxing divisions. ONE Submission Grappling uses long-format matches (10-15 minutes), all submissions legal, with judge-decision permitted if no submission occurs. Notable competitors: Mikey Musumeci, Shinya Aoki, Garry Tonon. ONE's format complements the ADCC biennial championship + UWW Grappling federated competition by establishing a sustained professional circuit. See sibling ADCC Rules and UWW Grappling Rules entries for related organizations.

Section 2: Equipment

  • No-gi only: rashguard + shorts (or grappling pants); rashguard must cover torso
  • Mouthguard recommended; protective cup permitted (men)
  • No shoes, no jewelry, nails trimmed, hair tied back
  • Athletes typically wear branded ONE-branded kit (sponsor-coordinated)

Section 5: Rules of Play

Match Length

  • Super fight: 10 minutes (single period)
  • Title fight: 12 minutes (single period)
  • If tied: judge decision (no overtime golden-score)

Permitted Submissions

  • All joint locks (arms + legs)
  • All chokes
  • Heel hooks fully legal at all weight classes (atomic-grappling-style permissive ruleset)
  • Twisters + neck cranks permitted

Pulling Guard

  • Pulling guard is permitted (not penalized as in ADCC); ONE values active offense from any position
  • Stalling is penalized regardless of position

Win Conditions

  • Submission via tap = total victory
  • Time expiration → judge decision based on aggression, dominance, submission attempts, control time

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Stalling: warning → point deduction → DQ
  • Slamming: DQ
  • Eye-poke, fish-hooking, biting: DQ
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: DQ
  • Refusal to fight / faking injury: point deduction or DQ
  • Anti-doping: ONE Championship anti-doping policy (WADA-aligned); athletes subject to year-round testing

Section 8: Safety Considerations

All-submissions-legal format including heel hooks places ONE's grappling division at the higher-risk end of competitive grappling. Mandatory comprehensive pre-event medical screening (cardiac, neurological, ophthalmological). On-site team physician + EMT + ambulance + AED. Concussion protocol per ONE medical guidelines. Slamming prohibited. Skin-infection screening before mat use. ONE's hydration testing + day-of-fight weight policy applies to grappling as well as MMA — IV-fluid use prohibited. Judges have stoppage-recommendation authority for safety even before referee acts.
