Section 1: Introduction

The Abu Dhabi Combat Club (ADCC) Submission Wrestling World Championship is the most prestigious no-gi submission grappling event in the world. Founded in 1998 by Sheikh Tahnoun bin Zayed Al Nahyan of the UAE royal family to identify the world's best grappler regardless of style (BJJ, wrestling, judo, sambo, catch wrestling), ADCC is held every two years (Las Vegas-based since 2022). ADCC's combat-realistic ruleset emphasizes submission attempts over guard pulling — all submissions including heel hooks are legal in every weight class, and prolonged stalling or "pulling guard" produces negative points. See sibling UWW Grappling Rules entry for the alternative international (Olympic-recognized) ruleset.

Section 2: Equipment

  • No-gi only: rashguard + shorts (no boards shorts, no pockets)
  • Rashguard must cover torso; no zippers
  • Mouthguard recommended; protective cup permitted (men); women: chest guard optional
  • No shoes, no jewelry, nails trimmed, hair tied back
  • Match attire color: typically white/black-belt rashguard or competition kit by organizer

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Mat: 10m × 10m competition mat (larger than IBJJF standard 8m)
  • Padded outer safety zone
  • Center line + marking for restart positioning
  • Single championship mat at finals; multiple mats during qualification rounds

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Two competitors per match
  • Weight classes (Adult Male, kg): -66, -77, -88, -99, +99
  • Weight classes (Adult Female, kg): -60, +60
  • Absolute (open weight): all male competitors compete together for absolute championship
  • Officials: 1 mat referee + 2 corner judges + scoring official + timekeeper

Section 5: Rules of Play

Match Length (Open Class / Championship)

  • Total: 10 minutes (qualifiers); 15 minutes (semifinals + final)
  • First 5 minutes: no points awarded — pure submission hunt
  • Second 5 minutes (and any overtime): points awarded for positional dominance

Points (when applicable)

  • 4 points: Mount, Back Mount, Mounted/Body Triangle
  • 3 points: Passing Guard
  • 2 points: Knee on Belly, Sweep
  • 2 points: Takedown ending in dominant position

Submissions

  • All submissions legal in all categories (including heel hooks, knee bars, neck cranks, twisters)
  • Submission via verbal/tap = instant win

Overtime

  • If tied after regulation: sudden-death overtime (variable format by edition)
  • Common: extra time period focused on submission attempts; or referee decision based on aggression + activity

Section 6: Scoring

  • Decision priority: submission > points > referee decision (based on aggression, activity, near-finishes)
  • Tournament: single-elimination bracket per weight class + absolute
  • Gold/Silver/Bronze per weight + Absolute champion separately
  • Trial-system: ADCC East/West/North/South + Asia/Oceania/Europe trials feed Worlds

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Stalling: verbal warning → −1 point (negative); continued stalling can result in DQ
  • Pulling guard (in regulation): −1 point automatically (designed to penalize passive play)
  • Fleeing match area: −1 point
  • Slamming: DQ
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: warning → DQ
  • Intentional injury: DQ
  • Anti-doping: ADCC anti-doping policy at championship level; WADA-aligned testing

Section 8: Safety Considerations

ADCC allows the full spectrum of submissions including high-injury-risk techniques (heel hooks, twisters). Athletes must demonstrate qualification + experience to compete (Trials system filters competitors). Mandatory pre-event medical screening. On-site team physician + EMT + ambulance for championship event. AED on-site. Skin-infection screening. Slamming prohibited. Referee authority to stop matches for safety. Concussion protocol per ADCC medical guidelines — head-impact assessment + return-to-competition clearance. Athletes responsible for verbal/tap submission timing — late taps can result in completed injuries.
