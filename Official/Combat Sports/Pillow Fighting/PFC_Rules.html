Section 1: Introduction

Pillow Fight Championship (PFC) is a professional combat-sports promotion that turns the childhood game of pillow-fighting into a regulated, broadcasted, prize-purse competition. Founded in 2022, PFC fights feature weighted regulation pillows (~2 lb / 1 kg), 3 rounds × 90 seconds, and full-contact combat with KO and judges' decision both possible. The format went viral on social media in 2022-2023 and quickly attracted former boxers, MMA fighters, and martial-artists looking for lower-impact striking competition with higher entertainment value than traditional combat sports.

Section 2: Equipment

  • PFC regulation pillow: ~2 lb (1 kg) weighted pillow with reinforced cover — same model issued to both competitors pre-fight
  • Mouthguard: mandatory
  • Groin protector: mandatory (men); chest protector for women
  • No gloves (pillow serves as the striking implement; bare-hand grip on pillow handle)
  • MMA-style shorts or boxing trunks
  • Barefoot or wrestling-shoe footwear; no street shoes

Section 5: Rules of Play

Match Length

  • 3 rounds × 90 seconds (60-second rest between rounds)
  • Championship fights: 3 or 5 rounds (event-dependent)

Permitted Strikes

  • Pillow strikes only — swing the pillow at opponent's head + body
  • Two-handed pillow grip permitted
  • Spinning pillow strikes legal
  • Quick combinations + body shots both score

Prohibited Techniques

  • No closed-fist punches without pillow
  • No kicks, knees, elbows, head-butts
  • No takedowns, grappling, or ground fighting
  • No striking with pillow already dropped (must regain grip)
  • No striking opponent while they are on the canvas

Knockdowns + Knockouts

  • Standing 10-count for knockdowns (rare but possible from accumulated body shots + dehydration)
  • Three-knockdown rule per round = TKO
  • KO = total victory; TKO via referee/doctor stoppage
  • Pillow-loss: if a fighter drops their pillow, they have 5 seconds to recover it before referee restart

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Striking without pillow (closed fist / kick / etc.): warning → point deduction → DQ
  • Hitting opponent while down: point deduction → DQ
  • Hitting after the bell: point deduction
  • Faking injury / refusing to engage: point deduction
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: DQ
  • Anti-doping: state athletic commission (where applicable) + PFC anti-doping policy at championship level

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Pillow Fight Championship dramatically reduces concussion + KO risk vs traditional combat sports due to the pillow's impact-spreading surface. Injuries are typically: shoulder strain (overuse), dehydration in long fights, occasional accidental finger-bend on pillow grip. PFC still mandates: pre-fight medical (neurological, cardiac), ringside physician with stoppage authority, on-site EMT + ambulance, AED in arena. Post-fight medical assessment. KOs (rare) trigger automatic medical suspension (minimum 14-day rest, neurological clearance required). State athletic commission oversight at events held in commission-regulated jurisdictions. Concussion protocol per ringside physician guidelines.
