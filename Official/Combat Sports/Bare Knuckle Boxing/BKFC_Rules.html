Section 1: Introduction

Bare Knuckle Fighting Championship (BKFC) is the largest and most established state-sanctioned bare-knuckle boxing promotion in the world. Founded in 2018 by David Feldman, BKFC held its first event in Wyoming under a state-sanctioned regulatory framework — the first legal bare-knuckle promotion in the U.S. since the 19th century. BKFC is now sanctioned in 30+ U.S. states plus internationally. The promotion's "BKFC Unified Rules" combine wrapped-wrist (no glove) striking with boxing-style scoring and a distinctive starting position called the "Toe-the-Line". BKFC has grown rapidly as a PPV combat-sports property, attracting fighters from MMA, boxing, and other combat backgrounds.

Section 2: Equipment

  • No gloves — hands wrapped from approximately 1 inch above the wrist to mid-knuckle with gauze + tape (no padding over knuckles)
  • Hand wrap inspection: commission-certified inspection pre-fight
  • Mouthguard: mandatory
  • Groin protector: mandatory (men); chest protector for women
  • Footwear: shoes prohibited; competitors fight barefoot
  • Trunks: standard boxing-style trunks; women: combat-sports approved tops

Section 3: Playing Area

  • BKFC Ring (octagonal "Squared Circle"): circular fighting area with 4 sides, smaller than boxing ring (~22 ft diameter)
  • Two parallel lines drawn at center of the ring marking the "Toe-the-Line" starting position
  • Padded ring floor; turnbuckle padding on corner posts

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Two competitors per match
  • Weight classes (Men, lb): Strawweight (-115), Flyweight (-125), Bantamweight (-135), Featherweight (-145), Lightweight (-155), Welterweight (-165), Middleweight (-175), Light Heavyweight (-185), Cruiserweight (-205), Heavyweight (+205)
  • Weight classes (Women): Atomweight, Strawweight, Flyweight, Bantamweight, Featherweight
  • Officials: 1 ring referee + 3 judges + ringside physician + commission inspector

Section 5: Rules of Play

Match Length

  • 5 rounds × 2 minutes (60-second rest between rounds)
  • Championship fights: 5 rounds (unchanged) or 7 rounds for some title contests

Toe-the-Line Start

  • Each round begins with competitors at center, toes on parallel marked lines, hands at shoulder level — referee calls "Knuckle Up"
  • This distinctive start eliminates running/circling for the first contact of each round

Permitted Strikes

  • Punches with closed fist (any portion of hand, given no glove)
  • Elbows: not permitted in BKFC (distinct from full MMA-style bare-knuckle alternatives)
  • No kicks, knees, head-butts, takedowns
  • Clinch permitted but referee separates if no productive action within ~5 seconds

Knockdowns + Knockouts

  • Standard 10-count from referee for knockdowns
  • 3-knockdown rule per round: 3 knockdowns in one round = TKO
  • KO = total victory; TKO via referee/doctor stoppage = total victory

Section 6: Scoring

  • 10-Point Must scoring system (standard boxing-style)
  • 3 judges score independently per round
  • Draw decisions possible; majority/unanimous/split decision outcomes
  • Titles: BKFC champion + interim champion per weight class
  • BKFC Knucklemania PPV events feature title fights

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Illegal strikes (elbows, kicks, head-butts, back-of-head, throat, spine, eye-poke): point deduction → DQ
  • Hitting after the bell: point deduction
  • Holding-and-hitting: referee warning → point deduction
  • Excessive grappling: ref break-up; no points; eventually warning
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct / corner-man interference: point deduction → DQ
  • Anti-doping: state athletic commission requirements (VADA collaboration at many events); WADA-aligned testing for championship fights

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Bare-knuckle striking produces more facial cuts and lacerations than gloved boxing but reportedly fewer concussions (because punches require pulling slightly to protect the puncher's hand). BKFC mandates: comprehensive pre-fight medical (neurological, cardiac, ophthalmological, blood work, MRI for major bouts), ringside physician with cut/concussion authority to stop fights, on-site EMT + ambulance, AED in arena. Post-fight medical assessment is mandatory. Knockouts trigger automatic medical suspension (minimum 30-day rest, longer for severe KOs, with neurological clearance required). State athletic commission oversight at every BKFC event in the U.S. Concussion protocol: VADA + commission medical monitoring; SCAT-5 in some jurisdictions. Cut-stoppage authority lies with the ringside physician.
