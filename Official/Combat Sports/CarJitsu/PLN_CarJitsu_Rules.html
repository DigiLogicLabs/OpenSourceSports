Section 1: Introduction

CarJitsu is a novelty submission-grappling format pioneered by promoter Vik Mikheev under the Pro League Network (PLN) brand. The sport's defining innovation: matches take place inside the cabin of a stationary 4-door sedan, with competitors changing seat positions between rounds. CarJitsu's confined, claustrophobic format produces a uniquely creative grappling environment that rewards small-space technique adaptation (no traditional sprawls, limited mount possibilities, modified guard work). The format went viral on social media in 2022-2023 and has produced multiple championship cycles. Conceptually rooted in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu — see sibling IBJJF Rules for the base sport.

Section 2: Equipment

  • No gi: rashguard + shorts (or grappling pants)
  • Mouthguard recommended
  • Protective cup permitted (men)
  • No shoes, no jewelry, nails trimmed
  • Athletes must agree to vehicle waiver covering minor interior damage / non-targeted contact with vehicle structure

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Vehicle: standard 4-door sedan (typically pre-2020 model with separate driver/passenger/rear bench); approved makes vary by event
  • Vehicle is stationary throughout the match (engine off, parking brake engaged, wheels chocked)
  • Padded steering wheel cover for safety
  • Side windows down or removed for ventilation + camera angles
  • Interior cleared of valuables; non-essential interior components may be removed (cup holders, hanging air fresheners)
  • Surrounding "ringside" area: cameras, lighting, official station, medical station

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Two competitors per match
  • Weight classes: Lightweight (-77 kg), Welterweight (-83 kg), Middleweight (-93 kg), Heavyweight (+93 kg) — exact tiers vary by event
  • Officials: 1 in-vehicle proximity referee (positioned at open door for visibility) + scoring judge + timekeeper + medical observer

Section 5: Rules of Play

Match Length

  • 3 rounds × 90 seconds (approximately 60-second rest between rounds for seat-position changes)

Seat Position Per Round

  • Round 1: Both competitors in front seats (driver's seat + passenger seat)
  • Round 2: Both in back seat / bench
  • Round 3: One in front, one in back (cross-cabin engagement)

Permitted Techniques

  • All standard BJJ submissions (joint locks, chokes) legal
  • Heel hooks legal at Senior level
  • Position-based scoring adapted for cabin (no traditional mount/back; modified definitions)

Win Conditions

  • Submission via tap = instant win
  • Verbal tap or referee stoppage = win
  • If no submission across 3 rounds, judge decision based on aggression, positional advancement, and submission attempts

Section 6: Scoring

  • Decision priority: submission > judge decision (aggression, dominance, submission threats)
  • Tournament: single-elimination bracket per weight class
  • Title belts awarded per weight class within PLN CarJitsu

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Striking: closed-fist punches prohibited (no MMA-style strikes); some events permit "palm strikes" only — verify per-event rule
  • Intentional damage to vehicle interior beyond reasonable competition: warning → point deduction
  • Eye-poke / throat strikes / head-butts: DQ
  • Stalling / refusing to engage: warning → caution → opponent advantage
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: DQ
  • Anti-doping: PLN-administered testing at championship level

Section 8: Safety Considerations

CarJitsu's confined cabin presents unique safety considerations: limited referee access to interior, sharp-edge hazards from vehicle structure (gear stick, headrest brackets, seatbelt anchors), and ventilation/visibility constraints. PLN mandates: pre-match vehicle safety inspection, padding on hard interior surfaces (gear stick, dash, rear-shelf), open driver-side door for referee proximity (referee can reach in to physically separate competitors if needed), comprehensive pre-fight medical screening, on-site EMT + ambulance + medical observer with stoppage authority, AED on-site. Skin-infection screening. Heat-monitoring: ambient cabin temperature monitored (cooling between rounds if extreme); engine off prevents exhaust risk. Vehicle waiver covers minor interior wear; major damage requires repair fund disclosure.
