1. Introduction

Counter-Strike 2 (CS2) is a tactical first-person shooter developed by Valve Corporation. Two teams of five players compete as Terrorists (T) and Counter-Terrorists (CT) across a series of rounds. CS2 is the successor to Counter-Strike: Global Offensive and features updated graphics, sub-tick networking, and dynamic smoke grenades.

Competitive CS2 is governed primarily by Valve through its Major Championship system, with tournament operations handled by partners including ESL FACEIT Group (EFG), PGL, and BLAST. The game is one of the longest-running esports titles, with professional competition spanning over two decades.

This ruleset covers the standard competitive format used in Valve-sanctioned Majors and tier-1 professional tournaments.

2. Equipment & Technical Requirements

Hardware

  • Gaming PC meeting tournament specifications (typically high-end CPU and GPU)
  • Monitor: 240Hz or 360Hz refresh rate, 24-25 inch display (tournament standard)
  • Mouse: players bring their own (must be wired or tournament-approved wireless)
  • Keyboard: players bring their own (no programmable macros)
  • Headset: noise-isolating or in-ear monitors with white noise overlay at LAN events
  • Mouse pad: players may bring their own

Software

  • Official Steam client with Counter-Strike 2 installed
  • Tournament server running the current competitive build
  • No third-party software, overlays, or assistive tools permitted
  • Custom configs (crosshair, viewmodel, binds) allowed within game parameters

Network

  • LAN play required for all Major and tier-1 events
  • 128-tick (or sub-tick) dedicated servers provided by tournament organizer
  • Online qualifiers: minimum 50 Mbps connection, <50ms ping to server

3. Playing Area (Maps)

Matches are played on maps from the official Active Duty Map Pool, which Valve updates periodically.

Active Duty Map Pool (2026)

  • Mirage: Classic two-bombsite map with mid control focus
  • Inferno: Tight corridors and iconic banana/arch dynamics
  • Nuke: Vertically layered map with upper and lower bombsites
  • Ancient: Mayan-themed map with distinct A/B site approaches
  • Anubis: Egyptian-themed map featuring water-based rotations
  • Dust II: The most iconic competitive map in FPS history
  • Vertigo: High-rise construction site with elevator rotations

Map Structure

  • Each map has two bombsites (A and B) that Terrorists attempt to plant the bomb at
  • Maps are designed for 5v5 play with balanced T/CT dynamics
  • Spawn points, buy zones, and bombsite boundaries are fixed per map

4. Players & Officials

Team Composition

  • Each team has 5 active players and up to 1-2 substitutes (varies by tournament)
  • Common roles: In-Game Leader (IGL), AWPer, Entry Fragger, Support, Lurker
  • One coach per team; coaches may only communicate during timeouts and halftime (Valve coach bug rule)

Officials

  • Server administrators: Configure servers, manage match settings
  • Referees: Monitor gameplay, authorize tactical pauses, enforce rules
  • Tournament directors: Handle protests, scheduling, and rule interpretations
  • Anti-cheat staff: Monitor players and systems for unauthorized software

Player Eligibility

  • Minimum age: 16 years (most tournaments)
  • Must not be VAC-banned on their competing account
  • Must not be under an active ESIC or Valve competitive ban
  • Regional residency requirements may apply for certain leagues

5. Rules of Play

Map Veto

  • Teams alternate banning maps from the Active Duty pool
  • BO1: Ban-Ban-Ban-Ban-Ban-Ban, remaining map is played
  • BO3: Ban-Ban-Pick-Pick-Ban-Ban, remaining map is decider
  • BO5: Ban-Ban-Pick-Pick-Pick-Pick, remaining map is decider
  • Higher-seeded team chooses first ban or first pick (varies by tournament)

Round Structure

  • Each match half consists of 12 rounds (MR12 format, introduced in CS2)
  • Total regulation rounds: 24 (teams switch sides after 12 rounds)
  • Round time: 1 minute 55 seconds
  • Bomb timer: 40 seconds after plant
  • Freeze time: 20 seconds (buy period at round start)

Economy System

  • Players earn money through kills, bomb plants/defuses, and round results
  • Starting money: $800 (pistol round)
  • Maximum money: $16,000
  • Loss bonus increases with consecutive round losses (up to $3,400)
  • Team-based economy decisions (eco rounds, force buys, full buys) are a core strategic element

Tactical Timeouts

  • Each team receives 4 tactical timeouts per match (30 seconds each)
  • Coaches may communicate with players during timeouts
  • Technical pauses may be called for hardware/software issues (referee approval required)

6. Scoring & Victory Conditions

Round Win Conditions

  • Terrorists: Plant and detonate the bomb, OR eliminate all CTs
  • Counter-Terrorists: Defuse a planted bomb, eliminate all Ts, OR let the round timer expire

Match Win Condition

  • First team to 13 rounds wins the map (MR12)
  • If the score reaches 12-12, overtime is played

Overtime

  • MR3 overtime: 3 rounds per side (6 total per OT period)
  • Starting money: $10,000 per overtime period
  • First to win 4 rounds in OT (or equivalent lead) wins
  • Additional OT periods played until a winner is determined

Series Scoring

  • BO1: Single map win
  • BO3: First to 2 map wins
  • BO5: First to 3 map wins (Grand Finals)

7. Violations & Penalties

Cheating

  • Aimbot/wallhack: Use of any aim assistance or information-revealing software — permanent ban
  • Match fixing: Deliberately losing or manipulating outcomes for gambling — permanent ban
  • Bug exploitation: Intentional use of map exploits or game bugs — round loss, map forfeit, or disqualification
  • Coach spectating abuse: Exploiting spectator camera during live rounds — coach ban

Conduct Violations

  • Verbal abuse: Harassment of players, staff, or officials — warning to fine
  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: Excessive celebration, taunting, or disruptive behavior — warning to suspension
  • Betting violations: Players or staff betting on matches they can influence — suspension to permanent ban

Penalty Tiers

  1. Verbal warning
  2. Round or map forfeiture
  3. Monetary fine
  4. Tournament disqualification
  5. Temporary competitive ban (months to years)
  6. Permanent ban from Valve-sanctioned events

8. Safety & Integrity

Anti-Cheat

  • Valve Anti-Cheat (VAC) runs on all competitive systems
  • Tournament-specific anti-cheat clients may be required (e.g., FACEIT Anti-Cheat)
  • Peripheral device checks: mouse and keyboard firmware verified at LAN events
  • Player PCs may be inspected before and during matches

Player Welfare

  • Adequate rest periods between matches at multi-day events
  • Soundproof booths or noise-canceling setups at major LAN events
  • Access to medical staff and support services during events
  • Players must not be under the influence of prohibited substances during competition

Data Integrity

  • All competitive matches are recorded (GOTV demos)
  • Demo files are available for post-match review and dispute resolution
  • Server-side anti-cheat logs are retained for investigation purposes
  • ESIC (Esports Integrity Commission) may conduct independent investigations
