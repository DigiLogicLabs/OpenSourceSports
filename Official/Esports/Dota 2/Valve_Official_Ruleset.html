1. Introduction

Dota 2 is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed by Valve Corporation. Two teams of five players each select heroes from a pool of over 120 and compete to destroy the opposing team's Ancient, a core structure in their base.

Competitive Dota 2 is anchored by The International (TI), one of the most prestigious esports tournaments in the world, featuring prize pools that have exceeded $40 million. The professional circuit includes regional leagues (DPC), Major tournaments, and The International.

This ruleset covers the standard competitive format used in Valve-sanctioned events and tier-1 professional tournaments.

2. Equipment & Technical Requirements

Hardware

  • Gaming PC meeting tournament minimum specifications
  • Monitor: minimum 144Hz refresh rate, 24-27 inch display
  • Mouse and keyboard: players may bring their own peripherals (no programmable macros)
  • Headset with noise isolation for team communication

Software

  • Official Dota 2 client via Steam
  • Tournament lobby hosted on Valve servers or tournament-provided dedicated servers
  • No third-party overlays, assistive software, or unauthorized modifications
  • Custom hotkeys and settings within the game client are permitted

Network

  • LAN play required for Major and TI events
  • Online league matches: minimum stable connection with <80ms ping
  • Dedicated tournament servers with anti-cheat monitoring

3. Playing Area (The Map)

All competitive matches are played on the standard Dota 2 map, which is symmetrically balanced between the Radiant (bottom-left) and Dire (top-right) sides.

Map Structure

  • Three lanes: Top, Mid, and Bot — each guarded by towers and creep waves
  • Jungle: Neutral camps on both sides providing gold and experience
  • River: Central dividing line containing Roshan's pit and rune spawns
  • Bases: Each side has barracks per lane, tier-4 towers, a fountain, and the Ancient
  • Outposts: Capturable structures providing experience and map control

Key Objectives

  • Roshan: Neutral boss dropping the Aegis of the Immortal (extra life) and later Cheese and Refresher Shard
  • Tormentors: Twin mini-bosses dropping Aghanim's Shards
  • Towers: Must be destroyed in order along each lane to access the base
  • Barracks: Destroying enemy barracks upgrades your creeps in that lane

4. Players & Officials

Team Composition

  • Each team consists of 5 active players and 1 coach
  • Standard positions: Carry (Pos 1), Mid (Pos 2), Offlane (Pos 3), Soft Support (Pos 4), Hard Support (Pos 5)
  • Coaches may communicate during the draft phase and pauses only (not during live gameplay)
  • Substitutes allowed with advance notice to tournament organizers

Officials

  • Referees: Oversee matches, manage pauses, and enforce competitive rules
  • Tournament administrators: Handle scheduling, seeding, protests, and appeals
  • Technical staff: Maintain servers, PCs, and network infrastructure
  • Broadcast observers: Control in-game camera for spectators (separate from player view)

Player Eligibility

  • Must be registered in the Dota Pro Circuit (DPC) system
  • Must not be under an active Valve competitive ban
  • Regional roster lock rules apply during DPC seasons
  • Minimum age: determined by regional league rules (typically 16+)

5. Rules of Play

Hero Draft (Captain's Mode)

  • All professional matches use Captain's Mode drafting
  • Each team bans and picks heroes in an alternating sequence
  • Draft order: Ban-Ban-Ban-Ban — Pick-Pick-Pick-Pick — Ban-Ban-Ban-Ban — Pick-Pick-Pick-Pick — Ban-Ban — Pick-Pick
  • Total: 7 bans and 5 picks per team
  • Newly released or reworked heroes may be disabled from Captain's Mode
  • Each hero can only be picked once per game (no mirror matches)

Match Format

  • Group stage: Best-of-2 (BO2) or Best-of-3 (BO3)
  • Playoffs: Best-of-3 (BO3)
  • Grand Finals: Best-of-5 (BO5)
  • The International: Group stage BO2, main stage BO3/BO5

In-Game Rules

  • No intentional exploitation of bugs, glitches, or unintended interactions
  • Fountain camping or excessive stalling is subject to referee warning
  • Pauses: each team has a limited number of tactical pauses per match
  • Disconnections: pause granted, reconnection within a set time limit
  • All-chat is disabled during competitive play

6. Scoring & Victory Conditions

Win Condition

A team wins by destroying the enemy Ancient. Games have no time limit and continue until one Ancient falls or a team concedes.

Concession

  • Teams may call "GG" (good game) to concede a match
  • Typing "gg" in team chat initiates a concession countdown
  • Concession is irreversible once confirmed

Series Scoring

  • BO2: 2 points for sweep, 1 point each for draw
  • BO3: First to 2 game wins advances
  • BO5: First to 3 game wins
  • Side selection (Radiant/Dire) is chosen by the team that won the coin toss or has higher seed

Tiebreakers

  • Head-to-head record
  • Game score differential
  • Time rating (average game duration)
  • Tiebreaker match if all else equal

7. Violations & Penalties

Competitive Integrity

  • Match fixing: Arranging outcomes or betting on own matches — permanent ban
  • Cheating: Using hacks, scripts, or unauthorized information — permanent ban
  • Account boosting: Playing on another player's ranked account — suspension
  • Collusion: Cooperating with opposing teams to manipulate results — suspension to ban

Behavioral Violations

  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: Excessive taunting, all-chat abuse — warning to fine
  • Harassment: Directed personal attacks against players, officials, or staff — fine to suspension
  • Public statements: Defaming tournament integrity or leaking confidential information — fine

Penalty Tiers

  1. Verbal or written warning
  2. Draft penalty (loss of ban or time reduction)
  3. Game forfeiture
  4. Monetary fine
  5. Tournament disqualification
  6. DPC point deduction or season ban
  7. Permanent ban from Valve events

8. Safety & Integrity

Anti-Cheat

  • Valve Anti-Cheat (VAC) monitoring on all competitive systems
  • Player PCs are provided by tournament organizers at LAN events (no personal hardware for system units)
  • USB device whitelisting at Major events
  • Demo replay files recorded and archived for all matches

Player Welfare

  • Mandatory rest periods between matches at multi-day events
  • Teams must provide adequate living conditions for bootcamps and team houses
  • Players have access to mental health resources through their organizations
  • Anti-harassment policies protect all participants at Valve events

Competitive Environment

  • Soundproof booths or isolation setups at all Major and TI events
  • White noise played to prevent crowd sound leaking strategy information
  • Server-side logs retained for all competitive matches
  • Independent integrity organizations (e.g., ESIC) may audit competitive matches
