1. Introduction

League of Legends (LoL) is a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed by Riot Games. In competitive play, two teams of five players each compete on the Summoner's Rift map. The objective is to destroy the opposing team's Nexus, a structure located in their base.

This ruleset governs professional and organized competitive play under Riot Games' esports ecosystem, including leagues such as the LCK, LEC, LCS, LPL, and the World Championship.

League of Legends esports is one of the largest competitive gaming ecosystems in the world, with millions of viewers and professional players competing across multiple regional leagues.

2. Equipment & Technical Requirements

Hardware

  • Gaming PC meeting minimum tournament specifications (set by tournament organizer)
  • Monitor: minimum 144Hz refresh rate, 24-27 inch display
  • Mouse and keyboard (players may bring their own peripherals)
  • Headset with noise isolation for team communication

Software

  • Official League of Legends tournament client (provided by Riot Games)
  • All players must use the current tournament patch version
  • No unauthorized third-party software, overlays, or macros permitted

Network

  • Tournament realm servers provided by Riot Games
  • Minimum network requirements: <10ms ping to tournament server
  • Backup internet connections required for official events

3. Playing Area (Summoner's Rift)

All competitive matches are played on the Summoner's Rift map.

Map Structure

  • Three lanes: Top, Mid, and Bot — each connecting the two bases
  • Jungle: Area between lanes containing neutral monsters
  • Bases: Each team's base contains the Nexus, two Nexus turrets, and an inhibitor per lane
  • Turrets: Defensive structures along each lane (outer, inner, inhibitor, Nexus)

Key Objectives

  • Baron Nashor: Powerful neutral monster granting team-wide combat buff
  • Elemental Drakes: Four dragon types granting permanent stat bonuses
  • Elder Dragon: Late-game objective with execute threshold
  • Rift Herald: Summonable siege creature

4. Players & Officials

Team Composition

  • Each team consists of 5 active players and up to 2 substitutes
  • Standard roles: Top Laner, Jungler, Mid Laner, Bot Laner (ADC), Support
  • Coaching staff may communicate with players only during designated breaks

Officials

  • Referees: Monitor gameplay integrity, enforce rules, manage pauses
  • Tournament administrators: Handle scheduling, disputes, and rule interpretations
  • Technical staff: Manage hardware, network, and client issues

Player Eligibility

  • Minimum age: 17 years (varies by region)
  • Must hold valid residency for their competitive region
  • Must not be under any active competitive suspension
  • Must sign the official player agreement for the league

5. Rules of Play

Champion Select (Draft Phase)

  • Teams alternate banning champions (5 bans per team, 10 total)
  • Ban phase: 3 bans each → 3 picks each → 2 bans each → 2 picks each
  • No champion may be picked or banned by both teams
  • Teams must lock in their selection within the allotted time
  • Newly released or reworked champions may be disabled for competitive play

Match Format

  • Regular season: Best-of-1 (BO1) or Best-of-3 (BO3) depending on region
  • Playoffs: Best-of-5 (BO5)
  • World Championship: Group stage BO1, knockout stage BO5

In-Game Rules

  • No deliberate exploitation of bugs or unintended game mechanics
  • Intentional disconnection or game-disrupting behavior is prohibited
  • Communication: team voice chat only; no external communication during live play
  • Pauses allowed for technical issues (must be approved by referee)

Chronobreak (Game Remake)

  • If a critical bug occurs, referees may rewind the game to a previous state
  • Full game remakes are ordered for severe technical failures
  • Referee's decision on remakes is final

6. Scoring & Victory Conditions

Win Condition

A team wins by destroying the enemy Nexus. There is no time limit; games continue until one Nexus is destroyed or a team surrenders.

Surrender

  • Teams may surrender after 15 minutes with unanimous vote (all 5 players)
  • After 20 minutes, surrender requires 4 out of 5 votes
  • In tournament play, coaches may also request a team surrender during pauses

Series Scoring

  • BO1: Single game determines the winner
  • BO3: First to 2 game wins
  • BO5: First to 3 game wins
  • Side selection alternates between games (higher seed chooses first game)

League Standings

  • Win = 1 point (BO1) or full series win recorded
  • Tiebreakers: head-to-head record → game differential → tiebreaker match

7. Violations & Penalties

Competitive Integrity Violations

  • Match fixing: Arranging or attempting to influence match outcomes — permanent ban
  • Cheating: Using unauthorized software, exploits, or information — suspension to permanent ban
  • Account sharing: Playing on another player's account — suspension
  • Collusion: Coordinating with opposing teams — suspension to permanent ban

Behavioral Violations

  • Unsportsmanlike conduct: Taunting, BM (bad manners), or disruptive behavior — warning to fine
  • Harassment: Directed abuse toward players, officials, or fans — fine to suspension
  • Social media violations: Leaking confidential information, hate speech — fine to suspension

Penalty Scale

  1. Verbal warning
  2. Written warning
  3. Fine (varies by offense severity and league)
  4. Game suspension (1-10 games)
  5. Season suspension
  6. Permanent ban from competitive play

8. Safety & Integrity

Player Welfare

  • Maximum daily practice hours may be mandated by regional league regulations
  • Teams must provide adequate rest periods during multi-day events
  • Players have access to sports psychologists and support services
  • Anti-harassment policies protect players from abuse by fans, staff, or other players

Anti-Doping

  • Performance-enhancing substances (e.g., Adderall without prescription) are prohibited
  • Random drug testing may be conducted at major events
  • Violations result in suspension and potential prize money forfeiture

Data Integrity

  • All competitive games are played on isolated tournament realm servers
  • Server logs are recorded and archived for dispute resolution
  • Replay files are available for review by teams and officials
  • Riot Games reserves the right to review any competitive match for integrity violations
