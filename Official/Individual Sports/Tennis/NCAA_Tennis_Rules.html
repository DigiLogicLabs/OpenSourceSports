Section 1: Introduction

NCAA Tennis is the collegiate variant of tennis played at NCAA Division I, II, and III institutions across both men's and women's brackets, in the spring semester. NCAA Tennis is unique in collegiate sport for its dual-match format combining individual singles + doubles into a team result. NCAA Tennis uses the ITF Rules of Tennis as the playing baseline with NCAA-specific modifications adopted by the Intercollegiate Tennis Association (ITA): no-ad scoring, condensed format, doubles-then-singles match order, and team-vs-team dual-match scoring. The NCAA Tennis Championship is contested in mid-May. The current edition is the 2025-26 NCAA Tennis Rules Book.

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Court: 23.77 m × 8.23 m singles / 23.77 m × 10.97 m doubles
  • Net: 0.914 m height at center, 1.07 m at posts
  • Service boxes: 6.4 m × 4.115 m each
  • NCAA dual-match venue: typically 6 courts side-by-side for singles, with adjacent stadium court for the deciding match

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • NCAA team: 6 singles players + 3 doubles teams per dual match
  • Lineup: 1-6 singles in order of ability; 3 doubles teams
  • Officials: 1 chair umpire per court (or 2-3 umpires per court at championship-level), line judges or electronic line-calling, tournament referee, dual-match referee
  • Players self-call lines on courts without umpires (NCAA D1 dual matches without championship-level officiating)

Section 5: Rules of Play

NCAA Dual-Match Format

  • 3 doubles matches first (1 set to 6, 7-point tiebreaker at 6-6) — winning team gets 1 doubles point
  • 6 singles matches (best of 3 sets, no-ad scoring, 7-point tiebreaker at 6-6 in sets 1-2; super tiebreaker at 1-1 in sets in some formats)
  • Total: 7 points possible per dual (1 doubles + 6 singles); first team to 4 points wins the dual

No-Ad Scoring

  • NCAA tennis uses no-ad scoring: at deuce, the receiver chooses which side to receive the next point; that point decides the game
  • Eliminates traditional ad-in/ad-out cycles

Tiebreaker

  • Set tied 6-6: 7-point tiebreaker (first to 7, win by 2)
  • Match tied 1-1 in sets (some NCAA formats): 10-point super tiebreaker decides match (first to 10, win by 2)

Section 6: Scoring

  • Game: first to 4 points (love, 15, 30, 40, game)
  • Set: first to 6 games, win by 2; tiebreaker at 6-6
  • Match (singles): best of 3 sets
  • Doubles match: 1 set to 6 + tiebreaker at 6-6
  • Dual match: first team to 4 of 7 possible points wins

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Code violation framework: 1st = warning; 2nd = point penalty; 3rd = game penalty; subsequent = default
  • Common violations: ball abuse, racket abuse, audible obscenity, unsporting conduct, coaching during point
  • NCAA Sportsmanship Code applies; ejection + disciplinary review

Section 8: Safety Considerations

WBGT-based heat thresholds for outdoor play; mandatory water breaks at high WBGT. Lightning detection halts outdoor play. Court surface inspected pre-match. Concussion protocol: removal + graduated return-to-play assessment for any actual or suspected concussion (rare in tennis). Required: certified athletic trainer at every match; team physician + EMT at championship-level. AED + emergency action plan at every venue.
