Section 1: Introduction

NCAA Cross Country is the collegiate variant of long-distance running over outdoor terrain (grass, dirt, gravel, hills, woodlands), contested in the fall semester at NCAA Division I, II, and III. Each cross country race is a head-to-head team competition in which individual finish places are summed for the team score (low score wins). NCAA Cross Country uses the World Athletics technical baseline with NCAA-specific modifications around race distance, qualifying, and team composition. The current edition is the 2025-26 NCAA Cross Country and Track and Field Rules Book. The 2026 NCAA D1 Cross Country Championship is contested in mid-November.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Athletic shoes appropriate to the cross country surface — typically spike length max 9mm; some courses prohibit spikes
  • Team uniform with school logo, unique singlet/jersey numbers
  • Optional: gloves, sleeves, hats, sunglasses (no advertising restrictions per NCAA)
  • Race number bib affixed to the front of the singlet
  • Timing chip (transponder) on the shoe or singlet

Section 3: Playing Area

  • NCAA D1 men's race distance: 10,000 m (10K) at the championship
  • NCAA D1 women's race distance: 6,000 m (6K) at the championship
  • D2/D3 distances may differ slightly per division
  • Course: outdoor over varied terrain (grass, woodlands, hills); marked with stakes, ropes, signage
  • Course must include start, finish, and at least one observable mid-course point
  • Loops/multiple laps acceptable
  • Course must be measured, certified, and approved by the meet committee

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Team composition: typically 7 runners per team for NCAA D1 championship; first 5 finishers score
  • Substitutes (#6 and #7): "displacers" — affect opposing team scores but don't add to own score
  • Officials: meet referee, course officials at marked points, finish judges, recorder, timing crew
  • Pre-race instructions delivered at the team meeting
  • NCAA D1 Championship advances 31 teams (regional auto-qualifiers + at-large)

Section 5: Rules of Play

Race Start

  • All teams line up in marked boxes at the start; team box assignments published before the race
  • Starter announces "On your marks" and fires the starter pistol
  • False starts: rare in cross country (mass start); first false start = warning, second = team penalty

During the Race

  • Runners must complete the marked course; cutting the course = DQ
  • No outside aid (no pacing by non-competitors, no electronic devices)
  • Course marshals enforce the route; runners must obey marshal directions
  • Bibs must be visibly worn front and back at the finish

NCAA Championship Qualifying

  • Regional Championships: each of 9 regions advances qualifying teams + individuals to the National Championship
  • Auto-qualifiers (top regional teams) + at-large bids

Section 6: Scoring

  • Individual: lowest finish time / first across the line wins
  • Team: sum of the finish places of the team's first 5 runners (low score wins)
  • Tiebreaker: 6th finisher's place (then 7th, etc.)
  • Displacers: 6th and 7th finishers do not add to their own team's score but do push opposing runners' places higher

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Cutting the course: DQ from the event
  • Outside aid (pacing, electronic device, illegal coaching): DQ
  • Failure to wear bib: warning + correction; subsequent = DQ
  • Unsporting conduct: meet referee discretion, ejection + NCAA disciplinary review
  • Equipment violation (illegal spikes, advertised shoes/uniform): DQ

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Course inspection pre-race for hazards (holes, sharp objects, uneven terrain). WBGT-based heat thresholds for race modifications/postponement. Lightning detection halts outdoor competition. Medical staff stationed at finish line + at least one course-mid medical point. Concussion protocol: removal + graduated return-to-play assessment for any actual or suspected concussion (rare in XC but possible from falls/collisions). Medical: certified athletic trainer + EMT/ambulance + AED at every championship-level meet.
