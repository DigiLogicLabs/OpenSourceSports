Section 1: Introduction

NCAA Rifle is one of only two NCAA-sanctioned co-educational team sports (the other is Fencing). Contested in the winter semester at NCAA Division I, II, and III institutions; ~30 NCAA D1 programs as of 2026. NCAA Rifle uses the USA Shooting + ISSF (International Shooting Sport Federation) technical baseline with NCAA-specific modifications. The NCAA Rifle Championship is contested in mid-March. The current edition is the 2025-26 NCAA Rifle Rules Book.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Rifle: per ISSF spec for the discipline (smallbore .22 LR or air rifle 4.5 mm)
  • Smallbore: .22 LR rifle, max weight 8 kg, single-shot bolt action; iron sights or scope per ISSF
  • Air rifle: 4.5 mm pellet, max weight 5.5 kg, max muzzle velocity 200 m/s
  • Shooting jacket, pants, boots, glove per ISSF Equipment Standards (NCAA-approved jacket inspection at championship)
  • Ear protection mandatory (smallbore); shooting glasses optional
  • Spotting scope at firing line

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Indoor shooting range with multiple firing positions (typically 8-20 lanes)
  • Smallbore: 50 ft (NCAA spec) firing distance with 50-ft target
  • Air rifle: 10 m firing distance with 10-m target
  • Backstop bullet trap behind targets
  • Sound-dampened range with ventilation per OSHA
  • Range officer station + scoring/electronic-target system

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • NCAA team: 5 shooters per team in each event; top 4 scores count for team total (drop the lowest)
  • Roster: typically 6-12 athletes for NCAA D1; co-ed competition
  • Officials: chief range officer, range officer, target judges, scorer, equipment inspector
  • NCAA Rifle Championship: 8 teams + individual qualifiers advance from regional qualifying

Section 5: Rules of Play

NCAA Events

  • Smallbore 3-position (3P): 60-shot match — 20 prone, 20 standing, 20 kneeling
  • Air rifle: 60-shot standing match
  • NCAA Aggregate: smallbore + air rifle scores combined for team championship

Position Rules

  • Prone: lying on ground, rifle supported by elbows + sling
  • Standing: upright, no support beyond the body
  • Kneeling: kneeling on right knee, sitting on right heel, support via the left elbow on left knee

Time Limits

  • Smallbore 3P: 1 hour 45 min for 60 shots + sighters
  • Air rifle: 1 hour 15 min for 60 shots + sighters

Equipment Inspection

Pre-event equipment inspection at championship: rifle weight, jacket fit, boot height, glove specs all checked per ISSF Equipment Standards.

Section 6: Scoring

  • Each shot scored 1-10 (10 inner ring center)
  • NCAA scoring: integer scoring (1-10) — different from ISSF's decimal scoring (10.9 max per shot)
  • Match score: sum of 60 shots (max 600)
  • Team score: sum of top 4 of 5 individual scores
  • Aggregate: smallbore + air rifle scores combined

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Equipment violation (oversized jacket, illegal sling, etc.): warning + correction; subsequent = 2-point deduction or DQ per severity
  • Time violations: shots fired after time = DQ for those shots
  • Position violations: improper position support = warning; subsequent = score deduction
  • Cross-firing (shot at wrong target): 2-point deduction from own score
  • Range safety violation (muzzle direction, action open): immediate ejection from event
  • Unsporting conduct: NCAA Sportsmanship Code

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Range safety is paramount: muzzle discipline (always pointed downrange), action open when not firing, ear/eye protection, no live ammunition outside the firing line. Range safety officer enforces all safety rules; violations cause immediate ejection. NCAA-mandated AED + first-aid + emergency action plan. Lead exposure managed via ventilation (smallbore); air-rifle has minimal lead exposure. Shooters undergo periodic blood-lead level monitoring per NCAA + USA Shooting recommendations.
