Section 1: Introduction

NCAA Golf is the collegiate variant of golf contested by NCAA Division I, II, and III institutions across both men's and women's brackets, with regular-season tournaments and conference championships culminating in the NCAA Golf Championship in late May. NCAA Golf uses the Rules of Golf (jointly maintained by the USGA and The R&A) as the playing-rule baseline with NCAA-specific modifications around team format, tournament structure, accommodation rules, local rules, scoring, and pace of play. The current edition is the 2025-26 NCAA Golf Rules Book. For underlying playing rules see the sibling R&A Rules of Golf entry; for USGA championship application see USGA Championship Golf; for PGA TOUR see Golf-PGA TOUR; for KPMG Women's PGA see KPMG Women's PGA Championship.

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Tournament course set up by NCAA Championship Committee with the host club
  • Daily hole locations published before each round
  • OB by white stakes/lines; penalty areas yellow/red; GUR white lines
  • Course ratings + slope determine handicap-adjusted scoring
  • NCAA team format requires 4-5 players per team scoring per round

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • NCAA team: 5 players per team in stroke play; lowest 4 scores count per round
  • NCAA Championship match play: 5-player team; 5 individual matches per dual contest
  • Caddies: NCAA-specific accommodation rules (some venues / divisions allow caddies; others do not)
  • Officials: NCAA Tournament Director, rules officials on each hole, video review at championship-level

Section 5: Rules of Play

Format

  • Regular-season tournaments: typically 54-72 hole stroke play; team scoring
  • Conference championship: same stroke-play format, with conference-defined scoring
  • NCAA Championship: 4-day stroke play (72 holes) reduces to top 8 teams + top individuals; the top 8 teams advance to match play bracket

Pace of Play

  • NCAA pace-of-play policy similar to USGA: each group has a timing par; bad-time accumulation triggers stroke penalties

Suspension of Play

  • 1-tone air horn: normal suspension; 3-tone: immediate suspension (lightning); short+steady: resumption

Section 6: Scoring

  • Stroke play: lowest cumulative score wins individual; team score = sum of best 4 of 5 individual scores per round
  • Match play: holes won determines match outcome; team match = best of 5 individual matches
  • Tiebreakers: aggregate strokes; sudden-death playoff at championship

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Standard Rules of Golf penalties (1 stroke, 2 strokes, loss of hole, DQ)
  • One-Ball Rule breach: 2 strokes per hole, max 4 strokes/round
  • 14-club limit breach: 2 strokes per hole, max 4 strokes/round
  • Equipment standards breach (non-conforming): DQ
  • Pace-of-play: warning → 1 stroke → 2 strokes → DQ
  • NCAA Sportsmanship Code violations: ejection + disciplinary review

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Lightning detection halts outdoor play; 30-30 rule supplemented by professional detection. WBGT-based heat thresholds; mandatory water/cooling stations on course. Course safety: spectator management, sight-line management, "Fore!" warning. Medical: certified athletic trainer + EMT on site at championship-level; AED + emergency action plan at every venue.
