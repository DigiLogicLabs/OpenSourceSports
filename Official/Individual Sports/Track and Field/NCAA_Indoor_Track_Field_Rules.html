Section 1: Introduction

NCAA Indoor Track and Field is the winter-season collegiate variant of track and field, contested December through March on indoor 200-meter banked or flat tracks, culminating in the NCAA Division I Indoor Track and Field Championship in early March. NCAA Indoor T&F uses the World Athletics technical baseline with NCAA-specific modifications including indoor-specific events, banked-track rules, altitude-conversion adjustments, and NCAA Championship qualifying. The current edition is the 2025-26 NCAA Indoor Track and Field Rules Book. For the outdoor variant see the sibling Track and Field (NCAA Outdoor); for the international framework see World Athletics Competition Rules.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Throwing implements: shot put, weight throw (replaces hammer indoors); same NCAA-approved spec as outdoor
  • Indoor-specific: weight throw — men 35 lb (15.88 kg) / women 20 lb (9.07 kg) — replaces the hammer for indoor competition
  • Hurdles: men's 60m hurdles 1.067m / women's 60m hurdles 0.838m
  • Spike length: typically max 6mm for indoor (track surface dependent), no longer than 9mm
  • Personal pole-vault poles meeting World Athletics spec
  • Personal high-jump and pole-vault landing equipment per venue

Section 3: Playing Area

  • 200-meter oval track (smaller than outdoor 400m), 4-6 lanes typical
  • Track may be banked (curves rise toward the outer edge to allow faster running) or flat
  • Sprint and hurdle straightaway: separate 60m straight track or use of the oval straight
  • Field event areas: shot put circle, weight throw circle, jump pits, pole vault pit
  • Indoor venue must meet NCAA championship spec for ceiling clearance, jump-pit dimensions, throwing-cage requirements
  • Class A certification required for NCAA championship venues

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Officials: meet referee, starters, recall starters, finish judges, lap counters, field-event judges, implement inspectors, wind-gauge operators (when used)
  • FAT (Fully Automatic Timing) required at championship
  • NCAA D1 Indoor Championship: top 16 individual + top 8 relays advance per event from the descending-order list
  • Combined events at indoor: Heptathlon (men) + Pentathlon (women) — different from outdoor's Decathlon/Heptathlon

Section 5: Rules of Play

NCAA D1 Indoor Championship Events

  • Sprints: 60m, 200m, 400m
  • Middle/long distance: 800m, Mile (1609m), 3000m, 5000m
  • Hurdles: 60m hurdles
  • Jumps: long jump, triple jump, high jump, pole vault
  • Throws: shot put, weight throw
  • Combined: heptathlon (M) / pentathlon (W)
  • Relays: 4×400m, distance medley relay (DMR — 1200/400/800/1600)

Banked Track Rules

  • Lane assignments and lane-line rules apply with adjusted radius for banked turns
  • Stagger lines accommodate banked-track geometry
  • Stepping inside the lane on a banked turn carries DQ if it impedes another runner

Altitude and Conversions

  • Indoor altitude factor: NCAA publishes altitude-conversion tables for performances at high-altitude venues (5,000+ feet); marks may be wind-noted but timing is FAT
  • 200m vs 400m oval: NCAA accepts marks from both for qualifying, with conversion factors published

Section 6: Scoring

  • Individual events: lowest time wins (FAT to 0.01 sec); jumps/throws: longest mark wins; combined events: highest score per published World Athletics tables
  • NCAA team scoring: 10-8-6-5-4-3-2-1 (top 8 score); relays score double
  • Tiebreakers: photo finish for ties; second-best mark for tied jump/throw

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • False start: zero-tolerance DQ (consistent with World Athletics 2009-onward)
  • Lane infringement: stepping inside lane line on a curve, impeding another runner = DQ
  • Relay exchange violation: outside the 20m exchange zone = relay DQ
  • Field event: foot fault, leaving circle improperly, implement landing outside sector, javelin not landing tip-first
  • Jump: crossing takeoff board, knocking bar from standards (3 misses = elimination)
  • Equipment: unapproved spikes, prohibited apparel = warning + correction; subsequent = DQ

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Indoor venue safety: ceiling clearance for pole vault and high jump verified; throwing cages required for weight throw; sector marshals positioned. Pole-vault and high-jump landing-mat dimensions and minimum thickness per NCAA spec. Banked-track safety: handrail and edge-protection at outer track edge. Concussion protocol: removal + graduated return-to-play assessment for any actual or suspected concussion (pole-vault head impacts and field-event implement strikes are primary risks). Required: certified athletic trainer at every meet; team physician + EMT/ambulance + AED at championship-level. Emergency action plan with cervical-spine and head-injury protocols at every venue.
