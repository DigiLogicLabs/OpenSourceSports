Section 1: Introduction

The UCI Mountain Bike World Series is the integrated premier competition circuit operating under the UCI's overall MTB regulatory framework. Launched in 2023 via a multi-year partnership between the UCI and Warner Bros. Discovery Sports (WBD), the World Series consolidates the previously separate World Cup-level events for Cross-Country (XCO/XCC), Downhill (DHI), and Enduro (EDR) under a single calendar, broadcast package, and ranking system. The 2026 season is sponsored as the WHOOP UCI Mountain Bike World Series. For the base UCI MTB regulations, see the sibling UCI Mountain Bike Rules entry.

Section 5: Rules of Play

Format Structure

  • XCO round: single-mass-start, 4-7 laps, 80-100 min target winning time
  • XCC (short track): separate short-track race on Friday, used for XCO grid seeding Sunday
  • DHI round: seeding run + finals run; fastest aggregate time wins
  • EDR round: multi-stage timed format, 4-6 stages over 1-2 days, aggregated time

Series-Level Rules

  • Mandatory series-entry commitment (riders must commit to all rounds for full points)
  • Drop-the-worst scoring: best 7 of 9 rounds count toward final standings
  • Series leader's jersey (presented at each round)
  • Series-wide anti-doping testing pool

Section 6: Scoring

  • Round points: top-30 finishers earn points on a sliding scale (250 winner → 1 for 30th, typical scale)
  • Series standings: aggregate of best 7 of 9 round results
  • Series Champion: highest series total at season's end → awarded series jersey + integrated into UCI MTB World Ranking
  • Worlds Championship (separate event) still produces UCI rainbow jersey per discipline

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • All UCI MTB violations apply (course-cutting, outside assistance, mechanical doping)
  • Series-specific: missed round without prior medical certificate → forfeit of round points + potential next-round grid penalty
  • Broadcast non-compliance (refusal to participate in on-bike camera or interview) → series fine
  • Anti-doping: WADA Code; positive test in season → annulment of all series results + minimum 2-year suspension

Section 8: Safety Considerations

All UCI MTB safety standards apply (helmets, full-face for DHI/EDR, course inspection, on-site medical). Series-specific upgrades: dedicated medical helicopter on standby for DHI/EDR rounds; tighter weather-stop protocol (lightning radius ≤ 10 km halts racing); two-stage course evacuation plan per round; series medical director presides over concussion-protocol decisions. Mandatory rider safety briefings before each round. Riders re-enter rotation only after independent SCAT-5 evaluation post-concussion.
