Section 1: Introduction

Pro Pickleball under UPA-A (United Pickleball Association of America) governs the unified professional tour following the August 2024 merger of the PPA Tour and Major League Pickleball (MLP). UPA-A maintains its own paddle-approval list, ranking system, and rules variations distinct from USA Pickleball (amateur/recreational governance). Pro Pickleball rules largely inherit the USAP framework but diverge in paddle certification, scoring options (rally scoring trials), and event-format specifics. For the amateur and grassroots ruleset, see the sibling USA Pickleball (USAP) Official Rulebook entry.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Paddle: must appear on the UPA-A approved paddle list (separate from USAP-approved list); pro-only restrictions on core hardness and surface friction; PBCoR (Paddle-Ball Coefficient of Restitution) testing required
  • Paddle dimensions: combined length + width ≤ 24 in (61 cm); max length 17 in (43.18 cm)
  • Ball: UPA-A-spec perforated plastic ball (40 holes, 0.78–0.935 oz, 2.87–2.97 in diameter)
  • Indoor and outdoor ball variants; tour events typically use outdoor-spec for consistency
  • Apparel: tour-mandated branding requirements (player kits / sponsor patches)

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Court: 20 ft × 44 ft (6.10 m × 13.41 m) — same as USAP
  • Net: 36 in (91.4 cm) at posts, 34 in (86.4 cm) at center
  • Non-Volley Zone ("kitchen"): 7 ft from net on each side
  • Service area: between baseline and NVZ line, split into right/left service courts
  • Tour venues require professional-grade flooring + sight-line refereeing positions

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • Singles: 1 v 1 — most common in PPA Tour individual events
  • Doubles: 2 v 2 — standard pro doubles + mixed doubles
  • MLP Team format: 4-player teams (2M + 2W) play men's doubles, women's doubles, and 2 mixed doubles per match; tiebreaker dreambreaker singles
  • Officials: head referee, line judges (up to 6 at tour-level), referee chair, video review staff
  • Pro players ranked by UPA-A points system (replaces separate PPA + MLP rankings)

Section 5: Rules of Play

Service

  • Underhand serve below the waist; drop-serve permitted (USAP 2021+ rule adopted)
  • Service must clear NVZ; lands diagonally in opponent's service court
  • One-fault rule (no second-serve)

Two-Bounce Rule

  • Returning team must let serve bounce; serving team must let return bounce — then volleys allowed

Non-Volley Zone (Kitchen)

  • No volleys while standing in NVZ or with momentum carrying into NVZ
  • Foot fault if any part of body touches NVZ during volley

Scoring Format Variants

  • Traditional: games to 11, win by 2 (best of 3 or 5)
  • Rally scoring (MLP): point on every rally regardless of server; games to 21, win by 2
  • Dreambreaker (MLP tiebreaker): singles rotation, first to 21

Section 6: Scoring

  • PPA Tour traditional: best-of-3 to 11, win by 2; finals best-of-5
  • MLP rally scoring: games to 21, win by 2; match decided by aggregate match points across 4 mini-matches + dreambreaker if tied 2-2
  • UPA-A Tour Championship: $500K+ events with multi-stage knockout brackets
  • Player ranking: UPA-A unified points (combined PPA + MLP results)

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Foot faults: service foot fault, NVZ foot fault
  • Service violations: illegal serve motion, contact above waist, drop-serve infractions
  • Code violations: verbal abuse, racket abuse, unsportsmanlike conduct → warning → point penalty → game penalty → match forfeit
  • Equipment violation: paddle off the approved list → DQ from event + potential fine
  • Replay protocol for line-call disputes; video review at TV-televised events

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Pro Pickleball is a low-impact racquet sport; primary injury risks are ankle sprains (rapid lateral movement), Achilles strain, and shoulder/elbow overuse. Tour events require an on-site athletic trainer + AED + EMT. Court surfaces must meet UPA-A grip + bounce standards. Heat protocols for outdoor venues (water breaks, medical timeouts permitted between games). Concussion-evaluation protocol in place. Paddles undergo PBCoR testing pre-event for ball-launch-speed compliance — over-spec paddles disqualified.
