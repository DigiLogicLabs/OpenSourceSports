Section 1: Introduction

The NBA Creator Cup is an NBA-sanctioned basketball tournament featuring social-media creators and digital influencers competing in an officially-branded competitive event. Announced as a permanent NBA programming property in 2024, the Creator Cup builds on years of informal creator basketball content (YouTube channels like Jesser, Overtime Elite, Power Slap-adjacent shows) by giving creators a top-tier league venue with NBA-grade production, officiating, and modified competition rules. The format mixes traditional NBA basketball mechanics with entertainment elements (creator-specific draft, fan-vote influence, shortened game length). Creator Basketball is a distinct sport-row from the parent Basketball (NBA) entry — the base game is the same but rules, format, and broadcast intent differ.

Section 5: Rules of Play

Game Length (Modified)

  • 4 quarters × 8 minutes (32 minutes total) — shorter than NBA's 4×12-min format
  • Half-time + commercial breaks for broadcast
  • OT: 3-minute periods

Rules (NBA-Aligned with Modifications)

  • Standard NBA rules apply: dribbling, passing, shooting mechanics, fouls, free throws, 24-second shot clock
  • Modified: "creator possessions" — once per quarter each team can designate one possession as a "highlight possession" (player must attempt creative scoring move; if successful, +1 bonus point)
  • Fan-vote may select certain in-game challenges (slam-dunk competition, three-point shootouts between quarters)

Scoring (NBA-Standard)

  • Field goal: 2 points
  • Three-point shot: 3 points
  • Free throw: 1 point
  • Creator highlight possession: +1 bonus when successful

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Creator Cup follows NBA injury-prevention + safety standards. Pre-event medical screening for all participants (athletic + cardiac clearance). On-court athletic trainer + team physician at every game. Standard NBA concussion protocol: removal-for-evaluation, multi-stage return-to-play, mandatory rest period. Court inspection pre-game. AED courtside. Creators who are not in regular athletic training are encouraged to undergo additional pre-event physical conditioning + medical clearance to reduce injury risk vs experienced basketball players. Mental health support resources offered for high-profile creators dealing with public-facing competition pressure.
