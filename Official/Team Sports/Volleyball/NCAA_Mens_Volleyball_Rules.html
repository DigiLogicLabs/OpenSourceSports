Section 1: Introduction

NCAA Men's Volleyball is the spring-season collegiate variant of indoor men's volleyball, contested January through May at NCAA Division I and III (NCAA D2 men's volleyball is contested as a separate level). The NCAA Men's Volleyball Championship is contested in early May. NCAA M's Volleyball uses the USA Volleyball + FIVB rule baseline with NCAA-specific modifications around set scoring caps, substitution count, libero usage, and challenge framework. The current edition is the 2025-26 NCAA Men's Volleyball Rules Book. For the women's variant (fall season) see Volleyball (NCAA Women); for the underlying FIVB rules see FIVB Official Rulebook; for beach volleyball see NCAA Beach Volleyball.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Ball: spherical, leather/synthetic, 65-67 cm circumference, 260-280 g, FIVB/Molten approved
  • Net: 2.43 m height (men's standard, vs 2.24 m women's), 1m × 9.5-10 m
  • Antennas marking the legal crossing zone
  • Posts 0.5-1.0 m outside sidelines, padded
  • Player apparel: team uniform with numbers; libero contrasting jersey
  • Knee pads recommended; ankle braces permitted

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Court: 18 m × 9 m, divided by center line into 9 m × 9 m halves
  • Attack line: 3 m from center line on each side
  • Service zone: behind end line, full court width
  • Free zone: minimum 3 m around court (5 m at championship)
  • Substitution zone between attack line and centerline

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • 6 players on court per team; roster 12-18 D1; one or two designated liberos
  • Libero: defensive specialist, contrasting jersey, plays only back row, may replace any back-row player without counting against substitution limit
  • Substitutions: up to 15 per set (NCAA framework); substituted players may re-enter
  • Officials: 1st referee (R1, elevated stand), 2nd referee (R2, opposite), 2-4 line judges, scorer + assistant
  • Video review: NCAA challenge framework allows review of ball in/out, antenna contact, foot fault on serve, net touch, and ball-handling per the published Rules Book

Section 5: Rules of Play

  • Best of 5 sets
  • Sets 1-4: rally scoring to 25 points, must win by 2 (no upper cap)
  • Set 5 (decider): rally scoring to 15 points, must win by 2
  • Service rotation clockwise on each side-out
  • Serve must clear net within antennas; let serve permitted
  • Each team max 3 contacts before returning over net (block does NOT count as contact in indoor — different from beach)
  • Side-switch between sets; teams switch in set 5 at 8 points
  • Two team timeouts per set (60 seconds each); technical timeouts at 8 and 16 points sets 1-4

Section 6: Scoring

  • Rally scoring: point on every rally regardless of who served
  • Sets 1-4 to 25 (win by 2); set 5 to 15 (win by 2); match won by first to 3 sets
  • Per-quarter team-foul reset framework matches NCAA W's Volleyball

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Faults causing loss of rally: ball out, four contacts, double contact (controlled), catch/throw, hand-setting violation, net touch, antenna contact, crossing centerline interfering with opponent, foot fault on serve, illegal server (out of rotation)
  • Misconduct: warning, penalty (yellow card), expulsion (red, out for set), disqualification (yellow + red, out for match)

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Concussion protocol: removal + graduated return-to-play assessment. Court surface and post padding inspected pre-match. Required: certified athletic trainer at every match; team physician + EMT at championship-level competition. Emergency action plan with AED access at every venue.
