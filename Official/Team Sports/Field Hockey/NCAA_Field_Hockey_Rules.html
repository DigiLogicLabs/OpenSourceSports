Section 1: Introduction

NCAA Field Hockey is the collegiate variant of women's field hockey contested in the fall semester at NCAA Division I, II, and III. NCAA Field Hockey uses the International Hockey Federation (FIH) rule baseline with NCAA-specific modifications around match length, substitution count, video review, and championship format. The NCAA Field Hockey Championship is contested in mid-November. NCAA Field Hockey is exclusively a women's sport at the NCAA level. The current edition is the 2025-26 NCAA Field Hockey Rules Book.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Stick: J-shaped, max length 105 cm, max weight 737 g, made of wood/composite; flat side only used to play the ball
  • Ball: hard, spherical, 22.4 cm circumference, 156-163 g, white with raised seams
  • Goalkeeper equipment: NOCSAE-approved helmet with face mask, throat protector, chest protector, padded gloves, padded leggings, kickers (overshoes), goalie stick (different than field stick)
  • Field player: optional shin guards, mouthguard recommended (mandatory at NCAA), goggles permitted/encouraged for safety, gloves permitted
  • Goal frame: 3.66 m wide × 2.14 m high × 1.22 m deep, with backboard (siding) 460 mm high

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Field: 91.40 m long × 55 m wide (FIH spec); NCAA may use slightly smaller fields
  • Surface: water-based artificial turf preferred (FIH spec); some NCAA venues use sand-based turf or natural grass
  • Two 22.90-m lines (originally "25-yard line"); one halfway line
  • Shooting circle: 14.63-m radius from each goal
  • Penalty corner mark: 9.14 m from goal posts
  • Penalty stroke mark: 6.40 m in front of goal
  • Goal frame backed by goal-mouth area

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • 11 players per team on field including 1 goalkeeper
  • Roster: typically 16-22 players for NCAA D1; up to 18 may be in match-day squad
  • Substitutions: unlimited and rolling (FIH/NCAA framework — distinct from FIFA limited subs); player exits, substitute enters at the 22.90-m line
  • Officials: 2 umpires, plus 1 reserve umpire at championship-level matches
  • Video review: NCAA framework permits video review of designated reviewable plays at championship matches (goals, penalty corners, cards)
  • Goalkeeper substitution: brief stoppage, additional padded protection allowed during transition

Section 5: Rules of Play

  • Match: 4 quarters of 15 minutes each (FIH adopted from 2014 onwards; NCAA aligns)
  • 2-minute breaks between Q1/Q2 and Q3/Q4; 5-10 minute halftime
  • Tied at end of regulation: NCAA Tournament — 7-minute golden-goal sudden-death OT periods, then penalty shootout if still tied
  • Stick play: ball played only with the flat side of the stick; raised hits forbidden except in shooting circle
  • Self-pass: free hit may be played to oneself
  • Penalty corner: defenders behind goal line, attackers outside circle until ball is played; high hits prohibited (ball must be below 460 mm of backboard) for the first shot
  • Penalty stroke: 1-on-1 shot from 6.40 m mark when defender commits a foul preventing a probable goal in the circle

Section 6: Scoring

  • Goal: ball wholly crosses goal line between posts, propelled from inside the shooting circle by an attacking player's stick
  • Goal from outside the circle = no goal
  • Most goals at end of regulation/OT/shootout wins

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Common fouls: obstruction, dangerous play, third-party block, ball played with rounded side of stick, foot kick, raised hit outside the circle
  • Card framework: green card (warning, possible 2-min temp suspension), yellow card (5-10 min suspension to penalty box; team plays short), red card (full ejection, team plays short for remainder of match)
  • Penalty corner awarded for fouls in the shooting circle by defenders
  • Penalty stroke awarded for serious fouls inside the shooting circle preventing probable goal
  • NCAA-specific suspension framework for accumulated cards

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Mandatory mouthguard at NCAA level. Goggles strongly recommended (high incidence of stick-strike eye injuries). Goalkeeper full padded equipment mandatory. Concussion protocol: removal + graduated return-to-play assessment for any actual or suspected concussion (head/face strikes from stick or ball are primary risks). Penalty corner safety: ball below backboard height (460 mm) for first shot reduces high-velocity head-strike risk. WBGT-based heat thresholds; lightning detection halts outdoor play. Required: certified athletic trainer at every match; team physician + EMT at championship-level. AED access + emergency action plan at every venue.
