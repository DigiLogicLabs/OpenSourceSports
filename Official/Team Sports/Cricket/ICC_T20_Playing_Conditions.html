Section 1: Introduction

Twenty20 (T20) is the shortest format of international cricket, with each side batting for 20 overs. Introduced as an international format in 2005 and as a World Cup in 2007, T20 is the format selected for cricket's return to the Olympic program at the 2028 Los Angeles Games — cricket's first Olympic appearance since 1900 in Paris. The ICC publishes separate Playing Conditions for T20 International (T20I) matches that modify the MCC Laws of Cricket — these playing conditions are the authoritative ruleset for international T20 play. Domestic T20 leagues (IPL, BBL, PSL, etc.) have their own playing conditions that broadly mirror ICC rules with league-specific exceptions (e.g., Impact Player, free hits expanded). For the long-format laws, see the sibling ICC Laws of Cricket entry.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Ball: white leather ball (4-piece construction) at international level; weight 5.5-5.75 oz (155.9-163 g)
  • Bat: max 38" length × 4.25" width; willow blade; max thickness 67 mm edge, 60 mm depth
  • Pads: batting pads, thigh pads, arm guards, helmet with grille, abdominal guard, gloves
  • Wicket keeper: additional inner glove + wicket-keeping gloves with webbing
  • Stumps: three wooden stumps + two bails, 28" tall, 9" wide at top
  • Players wear team-coloured kit (white-ball cricket uses coloured uniforms vs. whites in Test cricket)

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Field: oval; boundary diameter typically 130-170 yards (120-155 m)
  • Pitch: central 22 yards (20.12 m) strip of compacted earth/grass
  • Crease markings: bowling crease, popping crease (4 ft from stumps), return creases
  • 30-yard fielding circle drawn for power-play and fielding-restriction enforcement
  • Wicket at each end (3 stumps + 2 bails)

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • 11 players per side
  • Captain, wicket-keeper, batters, bowlers, all-rounders, fielders
  • Officials: 2 on-field umpires, 1 TV (third) umpire, 1 reserve umpire, 1 match referee
  • DRS (Decision Review System): teams have 2 reviews per innings (3rd umpire reviews via ball-tracking, ultra-edge, hot-spot)

Section 5: Rules of Play

Match Format

  • Two innings — each side bats once for 20 overs (or until all-out)
  • Each over = 6 legal deliveries; each bowler max 4 overs per innings
  • Match target ~75-90 minutes per innings; total ~3-3.5 hours

Fielding Restrictions

  • Power-play (overs 1-6): only 2 fielders allowed outside the 30-yard circle
  • After power-play (overs 7-20): maximum 5 fielders outside the 30-yard circle
  • At most 4 fielders on leg side at any time

Bowling Restrictions

  • Max 4 overs per bowler
  • No-ball: bowler oversteps front popping crease → free hit (next ball cannot dismiss batter except run-out)
  • Wide ball: outside off-stump (per umpire judgment) → extra run + re-bowled

Batting

  • Batter dismissed by: bowled, caught, LBW, run out, stumped, hit wicket, obstructing field, timed out, retired
  • Free hit ball: batter cannot be dismissed except run out

Section 6: Scoring

  • Runs scored by: running between wickets (1, 2, 3 runs per ball typical) or boundaries (4 along the ground, 6 over the boundary on the fly)
  • Extras: byes, leg-byes, wides, no-balls, penalty runs
  • Team total: sum of runs across innings (vs. wickets lost)
  • Tie: if scores level after both innings → Super Over (1 over per side; if Super Over also tied → another Super Over until decided)
  • Tournament: round-robin pool + knockout (T20 World Cup); Olympic format TBD

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Slow over-rate: fielding side penalized 5 runs to opponent per missed over; captain banned if persistent
  • Ball tampering: 5-run penalty to opponent + match-suspension fine for offender
  • Dissent / unsportsmanlike: match referee may issue suspension or match-fee deduction
  • Mankad (running out non-striker): permitted — recategorized from "Spirit" to "Run Out" in 2022; not penalized
  • Anti-corruption: ICC Anti-Corruption Code; full investigation + suspension powers
  • Anti-doping: WADA Code; ICC-administered testing

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Following the death of Phillip Hughes (2014), helmet standards for batters facing fast bowling were upgraded to British Standard BS7928:2013 — neck-protector ("Stem Guard") strongly recommended. ICC requires: certified match-day medical team (team physician + ambulance + medical center), concussion-substitute system (2019-onwards: like-for-like replacement if on-field concussion); player removal for SCAT-5 evaluation. Helmets mandatory for batters facing fast bowling, wicket-keepers standing up to stumps, and close-in fielders within 8 yards of the bat. Weather + light: match referee + umpires authorize stoppage for poor light, rain, or lightning. DLS (Duckworth-Lewis-Stern) method recalculates targets in rain-shortened games. Heat-stress protocols at hot venues. AED on-site at every ICC-sanctioned match.
