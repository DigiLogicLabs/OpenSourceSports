Section 1: Introduction

The International Federation of American Football (IFAF) governs international flag football, including the IFAF Flag Football World Championship (every 2 years) and qualification pathway for the 2028 Los Angeles Olympic Games, where flag football debuts as an Olympic medal sport. The IFAF 5-on-5 ruleset is the global standard for international play, differing in some details from the NFHS variant used in U.S. high schools. IFAF rules are administered through national governing bodies (e.g., USA Football for the U.S., GBAF for Great Britain). See the sibling NFHS Flag Football Rules entry for the U.S. high school variant.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Ball: IFAF-approved adult football (men's intermediate/youth size for women)
  • Flag belt: two-flag belt (one on each hip); flags 14-16" long, "pop"-style detach
  • No pads, no helmets — non-contact sport
  • Mouthguards recommended (mandatory in some national federations)
  • Cleats: rubber or molded only
  • Numbered jerseys; contrasting team colors

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Field: 50 yards × 25 yards (with two 10-yard end zones, total 70 yards) — shorter than NFHS
  • Marked midfield line for first-down advancement
  • 5-yard no-rush zone before line of scrimmage (defense)
  • Marked end-zone pylons + sidelines

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • 5 players per side on the field
  • Roster: 12 players per game roster (some events allow up to 15)
  • Substitutions: unlimited between plays
  • Officials: 3-person crew (referee, line judge, deep judge); 4-5 at World Championship + Olympic level
  • Replay review at IFAF-sanctioned championships

Section 5: Rules of Play

Game Length

  • Two 20-minute halves (running clock; clock-stops in final 2 minutes of each half)
  • 2-minute half-time
  • OT: alternating possessions from 5-yard line

Possession

  • Each possession starts at offense's own 5-yard line
  • Offense has 4 downs to reach midfield → fresh set of 4 downs to score
  • Coin toss for possession

Plays

  • QB must release ball within 7 seconds (some events 4 seconds for "no-rush" defense)
  • Forward pass only behind line of scrimmage
  • Defense rush only from 7+ yards back, or single designated rusher
  • Backward laterals permitted

Down a Player

  • Ball-carrier is down when defender removes either hip flag
  • Knee-down, ball-out-of-bounds, or flag falls off naturally = play dead
  • No contact: no blocking, no diving, no tackling — flag-pull only

Section 6: Scoring

  • Touchdown: 6 points
  • Extra point (PAT): 1 point from 5-yard line, 2 points from 10-yard line
  • Safety: 2 points (+ possession to scoring team)
  • Defensive TD (pick-6 or fumble return): 6 points
  • Tiebreaker: 5-yard line alternating-possession until decided

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Flag-guarding (offense): 5-yard penalty + loss of down
  • Illegal contact (offense/defense): 10-yard penalty
  • Defensive holding / illegal flag pull: 5-yard penalty + automatic first down
  • Pass interference (offense / defense): 10-yard penalty (+ automatic 1D for defensive)
  • Delay of game: 5-yard penalty
  • Personal foul / unsportsmanlike: 10-yard penalty; second = ejection
  • Stripping / horse-collar: 10-yard penalty + automatic first down

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Non-contact ruleset eliminates tackle-football collision risk. Penalty structure aggressively penalizes contact, flag-guarding, and dangerous play to maintain that profile. Mandatory mouthguards at most national-federation championship levels. On-site athletic trainer required at IFAF-sanctioned events; team physician at World Championship + Olympic competition. Concussion protocol mirrors World Rugby / IFAF medical guidelines — removal-for-evaluation + multi-stage return-to-play. AED on-site at all IFAF events. Heat protocols apply to summer-season venues (regular water breaks, heat-stop authority granted to officials).
