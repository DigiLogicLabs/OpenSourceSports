Section 1: Introduction

Ultimate (often colloquially "Ultimate Frisbee") is a non-contact, self-officiated team sport played with a flying disc. USA Ultimate (USAU) is the recognized national governing body in the United States; the World Flying Disc Federation (WFDF) governs internationally and is recognized by the International Olympic Committee. The current standard ruleset is the USA Ultimate 11th Edition Official Rules of Ultimate, effective 2024-2026. Ultimate's defining principle is the Spirit of the Game: players self-officiate, call their own fouls, and resolve disputes peer-to-peer — even at the highest competitive levels of college and club championship play.

Section 2: Equipment

  • Disc: 175-gram flying disc; USAU-approved manufacturers (Discraft Ultra-Star is standard)
  • Cleats: rubber/molded only (no metal)
  • Jerseys: numbered, contrasting colors per team
  • Mouthguards optional but recommended
  • No pads, helmets, or other contact-protective equipment (non-contact sport)

Section 3: Playing Area

  • Field: 70 yards (64 m) × 40 yards (37 m) playing field, plus two 20-yard (18 m) end zones
  • Total: 110 yards × 40 yards
  • Marked sidelines and end-zone lines
  • "Brick" mark 20 yards into end zone (for pulls landing out-of-bounds)
  • Field marshal stations at each sideline at championship level

Section 4: Players & Officials

  • 7 players per side on the field (standard outdoor format)
  • Roster: typically 14-25 per team; unlimited substitutions between points
  • Self-officiated at most levels: players call own fouls, infractions, and out-of-bounds; disputes resolved by discussion + re-do
  • "Observers" (USAU-trained, non-binding consultative role) at championships
  • WFDF / USAU "Game Advisors" may be assigned to active officiating at international championship level
  • Spirit Captain designated on each team — promotes fair play, mediates disputes

Section 5: Rules of Play

Starting Play

  • "Pull": defensive team throws disc to offensive team to start each point (like a kickoff)
  • After scoring, scoring team pulls next

Disc Movement

  • Disc is advanced only by passing — no running with the disc
  • Receiver must establish a pivot foot upon catching (basketball-style)
  • 10-second "stall count" by the defender (marker) — thrower must release disc before count reaches 10
  • If disc hits ground, is intercepted, or goes out-of-bounds → turnover

Scoring

  • Score when a receiver catches the disc in the opposing end zone with full possession + in-bounds

Spirit of the Game

  • All players responsible for fair play, knowledge of rules, mutual respect
  • Even at elite level, players make their own foul/contest calls
  • "Contest" → re-do throw; "no contest" → call stands

Section 6: Scoring

  • 1 point per score (in-bounds catch in opposing end zone)
  • Game-to-15 standard for outdoor 7-on-7 (cap at 19, win by 2 if reached)
  • Hard cap (time): typically 90 minutes total game-time; soft cap notifies of approaching end
  • Half-time at 8 points (or time-equivalent)
  • Champions decided by knockout bracket at USAU Nationals (College, Club, Masters divisions)

Section 7: Violations & Penalties

  • Foul: any non-incidental contact between players; called by the affected player → "contest" or "no contest"; if contested, disc returns to thrower
  • Travel: moving the pivot foot after catching; disc returns to thrower
  • Pick: defender obstructed; defender call → play stops + resume at point of obstruction
  • Stalling: stall reaches 10 → turnover
  • Strip: defender knocks disc from established catch → foul + disc returns
  • Misconduct system (championship level): Yellow / Blue / Red cards (USAU/WFDF); accumulating misconducts → game suspension

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Ultimate is non-contact by design — incidental contact happens but intentional contact is penalized. Common injuries: ankle sprains (sudden cuts), hamstring strains (sprinting), shoulder/wrist (diving layouts). USAU recommends mandatory athletic trainer presence at championship-level events. Concussion protocol: USAU adopted comprehensive head-injury protocol mirroring USA Football / NFHS — removal-for-evaluation, multi-stage return-to-play, mandatory rest period. Heat safety: USAU enforces heat-index protocols at summer tournaments (water breaks, mandatory cooling periods, potential weather stops). Lightning protocol: 30-minute clear-sky resume window. AED on-site at all sanctioned championship play.
