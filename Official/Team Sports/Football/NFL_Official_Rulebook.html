NFL - Official Rulebook

Governing Body: National Football League (NFL)

Last Updated: February 26, 2025

Section 1: Introduction

This document provides a summary of key NFL rules. For the complete and authoritative rulebook, please visit the official NFL website: https://operations.nfl.com/the-rules/nfl-rulebook/

Section 2: Equipment

All players must use approved equipment for safety and fair play:

  • All players must wear approved safety gear, including helmets, shoulder pads, and groin protection
  • The ball must be made of approved materials and specifications
  • Uniform requirements must be strictly followed

Section 3: Playing Area

The field of play must adhere to official dimensions:

  • Field dimensions: 120 yards (110 m) long by 53.3 yards (48.8 m) wide
  • End zones: 10 yards (9.1 m) deep
  • Goal posts must adhere to the official dimensions
  • Field markings must be clearly visible

Section 4: Players/Officials

Team and official requirements:

  • All teams must have 11 players on the field at all times
  • Teams may substitute players between plays
  • Officials include: referee, umpire, down judge, line judge, field judge, side judge, and back judge
  • The referee is the final authority for all rule decisions

Section 5: Rules of Play

Core gameplay rules:

  • Games consist of four 15-minute quarters
  • Teams must advance the ball 10 yards within four downs to maintain possession
  • All offensive and defensive plays must be approved by the referee before they can take place
  • All players must be in legal position before the ball is snapped
  • No tackling or physical contact is allowed between players outside of legal football plays
  • No intentional handballs or hand-balling of the ball except by eligible players
  • No intentional use of the hands or arms to control the ball by defensive players

Section 6: Scoring

Points are awarded as follows:

  • Touchdown: 6 points
  • Field Goal: 3 points
  • Safety: 2 points
  • Extra point (kick): 1 point
  • Two-point conversion: 2 points

Section 7: Violations/Penalties

Common penalties include:

  • Offside: 5 yards
  • False start: 5 yards
  • Holding: 10 yards
  • Pass interference: Spot foul or 15 yards
  • Unnecessary roughness: 15 yards
  • Fouls and misconducts must be sanctioned by the referee

Section 8: Safety Considerations

Player safety is paramount:

  • Targeting and helmet-to-helmet contact is strictly prohibited
  • Concussion protocol must be followed for all suspected head injuries
  • Equipment must be worn properly at all times
  • Medical staff must be present at all games
  • Extreme weather protocols must be followed
