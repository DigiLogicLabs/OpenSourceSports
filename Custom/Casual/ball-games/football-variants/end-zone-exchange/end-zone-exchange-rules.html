End Zone Exchange - Game Rules

Last Updated: February 26, 2025

Section 1: Introduction

End Zone Exchange is a strategic and athletic game that combines throwing accuracy and catching prowess. Players alternate between throwing and catching to score points through skillful catches in a dynamic back-and-forth competition.

Section 2: Equipment

  • 1 football (standard size)
  • Field markers or cones (to mark throwing positions)
  • Optional: scoreboard or scoring device

Section 3: Playing Area

The game is played on a flat, open area with the following specifications:

  • Two marked points approximately 25 yards (23 meters) apart
  • Clear of obstacles in a radius of at least 10 yards (9 meters) around each point
  • Soft, level ground preferred for safety during catches
  • Optional: designated "end zones" extending 5 yards (4.5 meters) behind each throwing point

Section 4: Players/Officials

End Zone Exchange is typically played with:

  • 2 players (1-on-1 format)
  • Optional: can be played with teams of 2 players each, alternating throws
  • No official referees required, though players self-officiate

Player roles alternate between:

  • Thrower: responsible for making accurate throws
  • Catcher: attempts to catch throws for points

Section 5: Rules of Play

Basic Gameplay

  1. Players take positions at opposite throwing points
  2. Players alternate between throwing and catching roles
  3. The thrower must keep feet planted during the throw
  4. The catcher attempts to catch the ball, with different catch types worth different point values
  5. After a catch or miss, players switch roles
  6. First player to reach the agreed point total (typically 21) wins

Thrower Rules

  1. Must keep feet planted at the throwing point
  2. Cannot fake or pump-fake throws
  3. Must make a legitimate attempt to throw a catchable ball
  4. Moving feet during throw results in a 1-point penalty

Catcher Rules

  1. May move freely to catch the ball
  2. Must maintain control of the ball through contact with the ground for a valid catch
  3. For special catches (one-handed, jumping), must clearly demonstrate the technique
  4. For volley catches, cannot use hands for the initial contact

Section 6: Scoring

Points are awarded based on the type of catch:

  • Standard catch (two hands): 1 point
  • One-handed catch: 2 points
  • Jumping catch (both feet leave ground): 3 points
  • Volley catch (using body part other than hands first): Additional points as pre-agreed

Game is typically played to 21 points, with players needing to win by at least 2 points.

Section 7: Violations/Penalties

Common Violations

  • Foot fault: Thrower moving feet during throw
  • Uncatchable throw: Deliberately throwing out of reach
  • False claim: Claiming a special catch that wasn't performed correctly

Penalties

  • Foot fault: 1-point penalty
  • Uncatchable throw: Loss of turn, no points
  • False claim: Catch counts as standard (1 point only)

Section 8: Safety Considerations

  • Ensure playing area is clear of hazards and obstacles
  • Warm up properly before playing to prevent injuries
  • Avoid playing on wet or slippery surfaces
  • Take breaks during extended play sessions
  • Be aware of surroundings when attempting athletic catches
